You've probably already got a website. The real question is whether it's bringing you work or just sitting there like a business card nobody reads. For most trades — from [plumbers and electricians to roofers and joiners](/industries) — the site looks perfectly fine, yet the phone stays quiet. Learning how tradespeople can get more leads from their website comes down to a handful of practical fixes, not an expensive rebuild or a clever marketing trick.

Someone searching for an emergency plumber in Leeds or a reliable sparky in Bristol makes their decision in seconds. They're on their phone, they're in a hurry, and they'll ring whoever makes it easiest. The changes below are the ones that reliably turn that visitor into a booked job.

## Make it effortless to get in touch

The biggest leak in most trade websites is a phone number that's hard to find. If a customer has to hunt for it, they've already tapped back to Google and rung the next firm on the list. Your number should sit top-right on every page and be tappable on a mobile, because most local searches happen on a phone with one thumb.

Give people more than one way to reach you, and never force them through a twenty-field form. A short enquiry box — name, number, postcode, what's wrong — alongside a call or WhatsApp button covers almost everyone. A [website built around getting enquiries](/services/website-design) treats that contact step as the main event, not something buried on a separate page.

## Get found when people search for your trade

You could have the tidiest site in the county, but if it doesn't appear when someone types 'electrician near me', it can't win you the job. This is where local SEO earns its keep. Claim and fully complete your Google Business Profile, keep your business name, address and phone number identical everywhere they appear online, and gather reviews steadily rather than in one big rush.

Getting the basics right here often does more for a small trade business than any paid advertising. If you'd rather not wrestle with it yourself, our [local SEO service](/services/seo) handles the profile, the pages and the technical bits that help Google trust your site.

## Build a page for every job and every town

A single 'services' page trying to cover boiler repairs, bathroom fits and emergency call-outs across three counties will struggle to rank for any of them. Google likes specifics, and so does a worried customer at eight in the evening. Give each core service its own page and, where it makes sense, a page for each main town or area you cover — written properly, not the same paragraph with the place name swapped in. That's how you show up for 'boiler repair in Stockport' rather than hoping a generic homepage does the work.

## On a phone, speed beats looks

Most of your customers are searching one-handed, sometimes standing over a leak. If your homepage takes five seconds to load, a good number of them are gone before they've even seen your name. Big background videos and heavy image sliders look impressive on a designer's monitor and quietly cost you work in the real world.

Keep pages light, compress your photos, and check that every button works with a thumb. A site that loads quickly and reads cleanly on a small screen will out-earn a prettier one that stutters, every time.

## Earn trust before the phone rings

People are naturally wary of letting a stranger into their home. Your website's real job is to answer one quiet question — 'can I trust this person?' — before they ever pick up the phone. A few honest signals do that better than any amount of polished design:

- Real photos of your own jobs — a genuine before-and-after beats a stock image every time.
- Actual reviews from Google or Checkatrade shown on the page, not just a logo linking away.
- Trade accreditations such as Gas Safe, NICEIC or NAPIT, displayed where people can see them.
- A short, human line about who you are, how long you've been going and the areas you cover.
- Some pricing guidance, even a simple 'from' figure, so enquiries arrive already half-sold.

> A visitor who trusts you before they ring is a warmer lead than any paid advert can buy.
