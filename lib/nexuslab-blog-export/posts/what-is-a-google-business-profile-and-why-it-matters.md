If you have ever searched for a plumber, an emergency dentist or a decent Sunday roast near you, the results that appear at the top — the little map, the star ratings, the opening hours — are powered by Google Business Profiles. So what is a Google Business Profile, and why does it matter so much for a small business? In plain terms, it is your free listing on Google Search and Google Maps, and for a lot of local firms it brings in more enquiries than the website ever does.

It used to be called Google My Business, which is why you will still see both names floating about. Whatever you call it, it is one of the most valuable pieces of marketing you can own — and one of the most neglected. Here is what it is, what it does, and how to make yours earn its keep.

## What is a Google Business Profile, and why does it matter?

A Google Business Profile is the free listing Google gives every business so it can appear on Search and Maps. It holds your name, address, phone number, opening hours, photos, reviews and a handful of other details. When someone nearby searches for what you do, Google decides which few businesses to show them — and your profile is a big part of how it chooses.

It matters because most local searches happen on a mobile, often with real intent behind them. Someone typing 'barber near me' or 'boiler repair Leeds' is usually ready to call or visit, not idly browsing. A strong profile puts you in front of that person at the exact moment they want you, without paying for an ad. Get it right and you show up in the map results, look trustworthy at a glance, and make it easy to ring you. That is the whole game.

> For many local businesses the profile brings in more phone calls than the website — yet it is the thing owners spend the least time on.

## Where your profile actually shows up

Your profile is not one thing in one place. It quietly feeds several parts of Google that customers use every day:

Because it powers all of these at once, a well-kept profile works in the background while you get on with the job.

- The local pack — the boxed group of three businesses with a map that sits near the top of local search results.
- Google Maps — where people search directly for nearby services and tap through for directions.
- The knowledge panel — the information box that appears when someone searches for your business by name.
- Voice and 'near me' searches, where Google leans heavily on profile data to give an answer.

Most profiles are not broken, just half-finished. The usual culprits:

## Frequently asked questions

### Is a Google Business Profile free?

Yes. Creating and managing a Google Business Profile is completely free — Google does not charge you to appear on Search or Maps. The only costs come if you choose to pay someone to set it up and maintain it, or to build the website it links through to.

### What is the difference between Google Business Profile and Google My Business?

They are the same thing. Google renamed Google My Business to Google Business Profile a while back, so older guides and plenty of business owners still use the old name. Nothing about how it fundamentally works has changed.

### How long does it take to set up and get verified?

The listing itself takes a few minutes to create. Google then verifies that you own the business, which can take anywhere from a few minutes to a few days depending on the method. If you also want a website that matches, most of our builds go live within three to six weeks.
