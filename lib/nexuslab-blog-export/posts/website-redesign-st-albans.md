A **website redesign in St Albans** is often the quickest way to stop losing that first impression.

The good news is that a dated site rarely needs ripping up and starting again. Sometimes it does, but often the fix is smaller and cheaper than you fear. Here is how to tell what is actually wrong, what a redesign changes, and how to decide whether yours needs a light refresh or a proper rebuild.

## The signs your website is out of date

You do not need to be technical to spot a site that has fallen behind. Look for these:

- It is slow to appear, especially on mobile data around the Ver valley or on a patchy train signal.
- It does not fit a phone screen — you have to pinch, zoom or scroll sideways to read anything.
- The photos, colours or logo no longer match how your business actually looks today.
- There is no obvious next step — no clear way to book, call or ask for a quote.
- You cannot update it yourself, so the opening hours or prices have been wrong for months.
- It has quietly slipped down Google, and you no longer show up for searches near you.

> A dated website does not just look old — it quietly tells people you have stopped paying attention.

## A redesign might be smaller than you think

The word redesign sounds expensive, and it makes a lot of owners put it off for another year. In reality, a full teardown-to-rebuild is only sometimes the answer. Often the problem is concentrated in a few places — a slow homepage, images that were never compressed, copy that no longer sells, or a booking form nobody can face on a small screen.

Where the underlying site is sound, a focused [website redesign](/services/website-design) can fix the worst of it in a couple of weeks: faster hosting, a proper mobile layout, sharper words and a clear call to action. Where the platform itself is holding you back — an old system you cannot edit, or one that breaks every time it updates — a clean rebuild is the better long-term call. Either way, we work to [fixed prices](/pricing) agreed up front, so you know the cost before anything starts and there are no surprises halfway through.
