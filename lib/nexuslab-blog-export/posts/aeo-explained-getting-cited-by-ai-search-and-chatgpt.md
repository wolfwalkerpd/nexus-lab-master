## What AEO actually means

AEO stands for answer engine optimisation. Where traditional SEO is about ranking a page so people click through to it, AEO is about becoming the source an AI trusts enough to quote. The 'answer engine' is any tool that gives a direct answer instead of a list of links: ChatGPT, Google's AI overviews, Perplexity, Microsoft Copilot and the rest.

The difference matters because the behaviour is different. Someone reading an AI answer often never visits a website at all. They read the summary, absorb the recommendation and act on it. If the AI mentions three local firms and yours is not one of them, you have lost that customer before they even knew they were shopping.

So AEO is not a replacement for SEO, it is the next layer on top of it. The good news is that most of the groundwork overlaps, which we will come to.

- SEO wants a click; AEO wants a citation or a mention.
- Answer engines pull from content they can read, understand and trust.
- Being named in an AI answer builds authority even when nobody clicks.
- Small, specific businesses can win here because AI favours clear, relevant sources over big vague ones.

## Why this matters for small UK businesses

There is a fair assumption that AI search only affects big brands. The opposite is often true. When someone asks an answer engine a local question, it leans on specific, trustworthy information: your address, your services, your opening hours, genuine reviews and clearly written pages. A tidy local business can be far easier for an AI to recommend than a national chain with a bloated, generic website.

Think about how people actually search now. Instead of typing 'emergency dentist Sheffield' into Google and comparing results, they might ask their phone a full question out loud and take the first answer. ## How answer engines decide who to cite

No AI publishes its exact recipe, but the pattern across them is consistent. They favour content that is clear, specific, well organised and backed up by signals that you are a real, credible business. Vague marketing waffle gets ignored. Direct, factual answers get quoted.

In practice, that means writing pages the way a helpful person would explain things: a plain question as a heading, a straight answer underneath, and no hunting for the point. It also means your basic facts need to agree with each other everywhere they appear, from your website to your Google listing to any directory you are on.

- Clear structure: headings that ask real questions, with direct answers below them.
- Consistency: the same name, address and phone number everywhere online.
- Credibility: a real Google Business Profile, genuine reviews and an about page that shows who you are.
- Freshness: content that is kept up to date rather than left for three years.
- Plain language: short sentences an AI can lift cleanly into an answer.

## Simple steps to start showing up in AI answers

You do not need to rebuild everything overnight. A handful of practical moves make a real difference, and most of them help your ordinary search rankings too. Start with the fundamentals below, in roughly this order.

If your website is slow, messy or years out of date, that is usually the first thing to fix, because answer engines struggle to read a site they cannot make sense of. A clean, well-structured [business website](/services/website-design) gives every other effort something solid to stand on.

- Answer the real questions your customers ask, in plain words, on their own clearly titled sections or an FAQ.
- Claim and complete your Google Business Profile, then keep your hours and services accurate.
- Make sure your name, address and phone number match exactly across every listing.
- Encourage honest reviews and reply to them, so there is a real track record to point to.
- Keep a proper about page that states where you are, who you serve and what you specialise in.
- Update your content regularly rather than treating the site as finished.
