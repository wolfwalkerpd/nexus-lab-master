## Wandsworth searches happen on the move

Wandsworth isn't one place, it's a dozen. Someone standing outside Clapham Junction — one of the busiest stations in the country — searches very differently from a family browsing the shops on Bellevue Road, or a commuter heading home to Southfields after work. What they all share is the phone in their hand.

Most local searches happen on mobile, often with 'near me' attached, or with a neighbourhood typed straight in: Balham, Putney, Tooting, Battersea. Google reads those location signals hard. If your website and your Google listing don't make your area unmistakable, you stay invisible to exactly the people standing closest to your door.

## What the Google map pack is — and why it matters

When someone searches for a local service, Google usually shows a small block of three businesses with a map above the normal blue links. That block is the 'map pack', and for a local business it's the most valuable spot on the page. Most people pick from those three without scrolling any further.

Google decides who lands there using a few things you can genuinely influence:

- **Relevance** — does your listing clearly match the search? 'Emergency electrician Tooting' beats a vague 'electrical services'.
- **Distance** — how close you are to the searcher. You can't move your shop, but you can shape which searches you show up in.
- **Prominence** — how trusted and established you look, driven heavily by reviews, mentions across the web, and your own website.
- **Reviews** — recent, genuine ones that mention the service and the neighbourhood carry real weight.
- **Categories** — the primary category on your profile quietly does a surprising amount of the ranking work.

## Your Google Business Profile does the heavy lifting

If you only fix one thing this month, fix your Google Business Profile. It's free, it feeds the map pack directly, and most Wandsworth businesses treat it as an afterthought — a half-filled listing with an old photo and no recent reviews.

Fill in every field. Choose the most accurate primary category, add the services you offer, upload real photos of your premises and your work, keep your opening hours honest, and reply to reviews — the good and the awkward ones. A profile that looks alive and cared for signals to Google that you're a real, active local business.

That's a genuine advantage for smaller firms. You don't need to outspend the chains; you need to out-tidy the competitor down the road who set their listing up once and forgot about it.

> For a local business, a fully filled-out Google Business Profile often beats a bigger competitor's neglected one.

## Get your website out of the way

Rankings bring people to your website, but the website decides whether they stay. If it's slow, confusing or awkward on a phone, all that hard-won visibility leaks straight out again — and Google notices when visitors bounce back to the results.

The fixes are the familiar ones: a [fast, mobile-first website](/services/website-design) that loads in a couple of seconds on patchy signal, a plain headline that says what you do and where, and one clear next step on every page. It also helps to look at how [businesses in your line of work](/industries) present themselves online, because the search behaviour of someone hunting for a dentist is not the same as someone chasing an emergency plumber. Get the website right and your SEO has something solid to send people to.
