## The website mistakes that quietly cost you enquiries

Before we get to what works, it's worth naming what doesn't. When we run a teardown for a business around SW19, the same handful of problems come up time and again:

- It's slow on a phone. Most people find you on mobile, and a site that takes five seconds to appear has often lost them before it loads.
- The phone number and location are buried. If a visitor can't quickly see how to call you or whether you cover their corner of Wimbledon, they'll try the next result instead.
- It says everything and nothing. Pages of vague copy about 'quality service' with no clear reason to pick you over the business three doors down.
- It's invisible on Google. No [local SEO](/services/seo) groundwork, a neglected Google Business Profile and no mention of the areas you actually serve.
- There's no obvious next step. Smart-looking sites that never actually ask the visitor to call, book or send an enquiry.

## What a website that wins local customers actually does

A site that brings in local work does a few simple things properly. Get this working and your site keeps selling while you sleep, turning a late-night search into a Monday-morning enquiry.
