Your website might have looked the part when it launched. But sites age faster than the shopfronts on Bold Street, and one built five or six years ago can quietly work against you now. If you run a business here and have been weighing up a website redesign in Liverpool, your customers are usually the ones who notice the problems first: the slow load, the text that is too small, the contact form that never quite worked on a phone.

The good news is that a dated site is almost always fixable, and it does not have to mean starting from scratch. Below we will walk through the problems we see most often on local sites, what a redesign genuinely improves, and how to tell whether you need a full rebuild or just a tidy-up.

## Signs your Liverpool website is showing its age

Some sites look obviously old. Most just underperform in ways you have stopped noticing, because you see them every day and your eye slides straight past. When we review a local site, these are the tells that come up again and again:

- It is slow, especially on mobile data around the city centre, where a heavy homepage can take several seconds to appear.
- It was never really built for phones, so buttons are fiddly and visitors have to pinch and zoom to read anything.
- The design looks a generation behind the competitors on your high street or in your corner of the city.
- You cannot edit it yourself, so a new price or a set of opening hours means emailing whoever built it and waiting.
- It barely shows up on Google when someone nearby searches for what you do.
- Details have quietly drifted out of date: old prices, a team member who left, a phone number no one answers.

## You might not need a full rebuild

Here is the part that tends to come as a relief. If the bones of your site are sound, a redesign can be more of a refresh than a rebuild: a new layout, faster pages, sharper words and proper mobile behaviour on top of what you already have. Other times the underlying platform is the real problem, and a clean [website redesign](/services/website-design) works out cheaper over a couple of years, because you stop paying to patch something that fights you at every turn.
