## What websites for tradesmen in Luton need to do

Most trade websites fail for the same reason: they look fine but make the customer work too hard. Someone with a burst pipe in Limbury won't fill in a ten-box form. Give people the quickest possible route to you and the calls follow.

Think about the person on the other end. Their boiler's packed in, they're standing in a cold kitchen in Sundon Park, and they've got three tabs open comparing local firms. The site that gets the call is the one that answers 'can you help, and how do I reach you' in about five seconds. That means a few things done well:

- A click-to-call button fixed to the screen on mobile — one tap and their phone is ringing yours.
- A short quote or callback form: name, number, postcode, job. Nothing more.
- A photo gallery of real work — before-and-afters of bathrooms, boards, brickwork and extensions.
- Genuine reviews, each showing the Luton area the customer is in.
- A clear list of the areas you cover, from Leagrave and Stopsley to Dunstable and Houghton Regis.
- Fast loading and a mobile-first layout, because most local searches happen on a phone.

> The tradesperson who's easy to call beats the better tradesperson who isn't.

## Show your work — proof sells in Luton

Word of mouth still runs Luton. The difference now is that the recommendation gets checked online before anyone calls. When a neighbour in Round Green mentions your name, the next customer Googles you — and what they find decides whether the phone rings.

Photos do the heavy lifting. A gallery of tidy consumer units, finished en-suites and completed extensions says more than any sales copy ever will. Pair it with a handful of honest reviews, each showing the area the customer is in, and you've built the trust that turns a maybe into a booking.

It also helps to name the places you work. A line like 'trusted by homeowners from Barnfield to Leagrave' quietly tells a visitor you're local, not a national outfit routing their call through a call centre two hundred miles away.
