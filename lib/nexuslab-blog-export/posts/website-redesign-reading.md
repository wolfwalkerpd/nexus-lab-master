## Why Reading raises the bar for your website

Reading is a demanding place to be seen in. Perched on the M4 corridor between London and the Thames Valley, the town has a customer base used to fast, polished websites — people who commute into Paddington in well under an hour, work for the tech and insurance firms dotted around Green Park and Thames Valley Park, and expect the local electrician or dental practice to load as cleanly as the apps on their phone. Most of them are searching on mobile, often on the train home or standing halfway down Broad Street, and if your site makes them pinch, zoom and wait, they have moved on before they read a single line.

The competition has not stood still either. Whether you trade in the town centre near the Oracle, run an independent shop up in Caversham, serve the student crowd near the university, or cover the suburbs out towards Tilehurst, Earley and Woodley, there is almost always someone nearby with a newer, faster site quietly absorbing the searches that should be landing on you. A dated website does not fail loudly — it just leaks customers you never hear about.

## Signs your Reading business needs a website redesign

A refresh tweaks the surface. A redesign fixes what is actually losing you work. It is usually time for the latter when:

- Your site looks dated next to competitors and undersells how good your work really is
- It loads slowly, or looks cramped and broken on a phone
- You cannot update it yourself, and nobody can quite remember who built it
- You get visitors, but almost no calls, forms or enquiries come from them
- You do not appear when someone searches for your service followed by "Reading"
- Security warnings, dead links or a broken contact form are turning people away without you knowing

> If customers can find you but never get in touch, the issue is rarely your prices — it is a website that fails to earn trust in the first five seconds.
