Learning how to add online booking to your website is one of the highest-value jobs a small-business owner can tick off. When a customer can pick a time and confirm it in under a minute — without ringing you, waiting for a callback or playing phone tag — you win work you would otherwise lose. And a lot of those bookings land in the evening, long after you've locked up for the day.

The good news is you don't need to be technical to pull this off. Whether you run a barber's in Leeds, a physio clinic in Bristol or a boiler-servicing round across the Midlands, the process is broadly the same. Below is a plain-English walkthrough — plus an honest note on where it's worth handing the fiddly bits to someone else.

## First, decide what a booking means for you

Before you touch any software, get clear on what you're actually taking. A booking for a hair salon is a named service in a specific chair for 45 minutes. For a plumber it might be a two-hour arrival window rather than an exact slot. For a restaurant it's a table for a party size at a sitting. The tool you choose has to match how your business really runs, not the other way round.

Ten minutes with a pen here will save you hours later. Write down the answers to these before you sign up for anything:

- The services or appointment types people can book, and how long each one takes
- What they're booked against — a specific staff member, a room, a table or a piece of kit
- Your real availability, including breaks, travel time and days off
- Whether you take a deposit or full payment up front to cut no-shows
- How much notice you need, so nobody grabs a slot for twenty minutes' time

## Choose a booking tool that fits

There are two broad routes, and the right one depends on how much you already have in place.

For a solo consultant, a light scheduling app is often plenty. For a busy clinic with several staff, deposits and reminders, a proper booking system earns its keep. Pick the simplest tool that covers your list from step one — you can always upgrade later.

- **A dedicated booking app** — a general scheduler for simple one-to-one slots, or an industry tool built for salons, clinics or hospitality. You keep your calendar there and drop a booking button on your site.
- **A booking feature inside your website platform** — most modern sites can run a booking system directly, so the customer never has to leave your pages.

> Pick the simplest tool that covers your needs today. An over-featured system you never fully use is just another monthly bill.

## How to add online booking to your website, step by step

With your tool chosen, the actual set-up follows the same shape almost everywhere:

That last step matters more than any other. Most local searches happen on a phone, so if the form is awkward to tap or the confirmation never turns up, people quietly give up and ring a competitor instead.

- **Create your account and connect your calendar** so the tool blocks out times you're already busy and never double-books you.
- **Add your services, durations and prices**, along with any deposit rules and buffer time between jobs.
- **Set your real availability** — the hours and days you genuinely want to work, not the hours you feel you ought to offer.
- **Get your booking link or embed code.** Most tools give you both: a shareable link and a snippet of code that shows the calendar on your own page.
- **Put it on your website.** Add a clear booking button in your main menu, then repeat it on your homepage, service pages and contact page.
- **Test it as a customer** on your phone. Make a real booking, check the confirmation email arrives and confirm it lands in your calendar.

It's also worth keeping the whole thing tidy over time — updated prices, seasonal hours, new services. A little upkeep through a [care plan](/services/care-plans) keeps the booking flow working while you get on with the actual work.

### Do I need a new website to add booking?

Not necessarily. Most modern sites can take a booking button or an embedded calendar without a rebuild. It's only worth starting fresh if your current site is slow, hard to edit or doesn't work well on phones — in which case booking is a good reason to sort the whole thing at once.

### Will online booking work on mobile?

It should, and it must. Most local customers book from a phone, often in the evening, so test every step on your own mobile before you launch. If the form is fiddly to tap or the confirmation email doesn't arrive, people give up — a good set-up feels effortless on a small screen.

### How do I stop no-shows?

Two things do most of the work: take a deposit at the point of booking, and switch on automatic email or text reminders. Together they give people a nudge and a reason to turn up, and they cost you almost nothing once they're set up.
