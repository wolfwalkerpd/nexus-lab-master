If you are planning a new small business website, you have probably hit the same debate everyone does: Next.js vs WordPress for small business websites. Both can produce a good site, so the choice is rarely as dramatic as the internet makes it sound. What really matters is which approach fits how a local UK business gets found on Google, earns trust and turns a visitor into a booking.

So here is a fair, jargon-light look at both. We will cover what each does well, where each falls short, and when it is worth paying for a custom build rather than a template. No trashing WordPress, and no hype about the shiny new thing — just the trade-offs that affect your time, your budget and your enquiries.

## The quick version

WordPress powers a huge share of the web, and for good reason. It is a mature content management system with thousands of themes and plugins, so you can get a functional site up quickly and often cheaply. Next.js is a modern framework used to build fast, custom websites, and it is what sits behind a lot of the slick, quick-loading sites you probably admire. Neither is simply 'better' in the abstract — they solve slightly different problems, and the right pick depends on the job you need the site to do.

- **WordPress:** quick to start, a vast plugin library, familiar to most freelancers, and cheap themes if you want them.
- **Next.js:** very fast pages, tighter security, a cleaner mobile experience, and more control over design and search.
- WordPress needs regular updates and plugin maintenance; Next.js needs a developer for bigger structural changes.
- For a simple brochure site either can work — the real difference shows as your site grows and ages.

## Where WordPress still makes sense

WordPress is a sensible choice when you want to publish and edit content constantly without asking anyone for help — a busy blog, a regularly updated news page, a site you like to tinker with every week. The ecosystem is enormous, so when you need a specific feature there is usually a plugin for it. If your budget is tight and you are genuinely comfortable managing updates, backups and the occasional plugin conflict yourself, it can be very good value.

The catch is that convenience has a cost. Every plugin you add is more code to load, more to keep updated and more that can break or open a security hole. A WordPress site that is not looked after tends to get slower and more fragile over time — and that ongoing upkeep is exactly what busy small business owners rarely have the hours for.

## Where Next.js pulls ahead for a local business

For most local and small businesses — a dental practice, a plumber, a café, a firm of accountants — the website is a shopfront, not a publishing platform. You are not blogging every day; you want a fast, trustworthy site that shows up in local search and makes it easy for someone to call or book. That is where a custom [Next.js build](/services/website-design) tends to win.

Because the pages are built to load quickly and cleanly, they feel snappier on a phone, and most local searches happen on mobile. Fast, well-structured pages also give you a solid technical base for [local SEO](/services/seo), which matters when you are competing to appear in the map results for your town. There are fewer moving parts to break, and no library of plugins quietly ageing in the background.

> For a local business, your website is a shopfront, not a publishing platform — it should load fast, build trust and make booking effortless.

## Next.js vs WordPress for small business websites: the honest trade-off

Here is the fair summary. WordPress gives you day-to-day control and a low entry price, at the cost of ongoing maintenance and, often, speed. Next.js gives you speed, security and a bespoke design, at the cost of needing a developer when you want structural changes. Neither is a trap — they simply suit different owners and different goals.

The deciding question is usually not the technology at all — it is who is going to look after the thing once it is live. A cheap theme that nobody maintains will cost you more in lost enquiries than a well-built site ever saves you up front. That is why we build on modern foundations and include a simple admin panel, so you can still edit your own text, images and prices without touching a line of code.
