If you're deciding between **Wix vs a custom website**, you've probably had advice thrown at you from both directions. One side swears Wix is all a small business needs; the other insists you must have a bespoke build or you'll never be found on Google. The honest answer sits in the middle, and it depends far more on where your business is heading than on which platform happens to be fashionable this year.

We design and build sites for UK small businesses, so we're not neutral. But we'd rather you made a good decision than a rushed one, so here's a fair look at what each option gives you, and when it's worth paying for something built properly.

## The honest case for Wix

Wix is a drag-and-drop website builder, and for the right business it's a sensible starting point. If you need a simple presence online this weekend and you're happy to build and look after it yourself, it can genuinely do the job.

For a hobby, a side project, or testing whether an idea has legs, that's a reasonable trade. The snags tend to appear later, once the business grows and the site starts carrying real weight.

- Low upfront cost. You can start for free and pay a modest monthly fee.
- Quick to launch. A basic one-page site can go live in an afternoon.
- No developer needed. The editor is built for non-technical owners.
- A template for almost everything, from cafes to consultants.

## Wix vs a custom website: the trade-offs that matter

Once you're relying on your site to bring in real enquiries, four differences start to count.

**Performance and SEO.** Wix has improved, but you're always working within its structure. A [custom website design](/services/website-design) gives you control over page speed, clean code and the technical details that help you compete in [local SEO](/services/seo), which matters when most local searches happen on a phone and the fastest, clearest result tends to win the click. Nobody can promise you the top spot, but a well-built site clears the obstacles out of the way.

**Ownership.** This is the big one. On Wix your site lives on their platform, and you can't simply pick it up and move it elsewhere. With a custom build you own the site, the domain and the data outright, with no lock-in. If you ever want to change direction, or change supplier, you're free to.

**Flexibility.** As you add online booking, a shop, or a specific enquiry flow, a builder can start to fight you. A bespoke site is shaped around how your business actually works, rather than the other way round.

**Support.** When something breaks on a DIY platform, you join a help-centre queue. With a small UK studio you deal with real people who know your site and pick up the phone.

> The question isn't which tool is best. It's which one still fits when your business doubles.

## So which should you choose?

Here's the simple way to decide.

Choose Wix if your budget is tight, your needs are simple, and you're comfortable building and maintaining the site yourself. There's no shame in that; it's the right tool for a certain job.

Choose a custom website if your site is a genuine source of new customers, you want to be found in your area, and you'd rather spend your time running the business than wrestling with an editor. If enquiries matter to your livelihood, the build quality matters too.
