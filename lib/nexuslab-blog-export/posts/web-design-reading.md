## What good web design in Reading looks like

A website that brings in work for a Reading business tends to get a few basics right. None of it is flashy, and all of it matters far more than the design trends people worry about. Get these right and everything else — the colours, the photos, the layout — has something solid to sit on.

- It loads fast on a phone, because most of your local searches happen on mobile and often on the move.
- It says clearly what you do and where, so someone searching from Broad Street or Whitley instantly knows they're in the right place.
- Your phone number, service area and opening hours are obvious — not buried three clicks deep.
- Every page points gently towards one action: call, book, or request a quote.
- It reads like a real person wrote it, not a template with 'Reading' pasted over another town's name.

> A pretty website that nobody contacts is just an expensive business card.
