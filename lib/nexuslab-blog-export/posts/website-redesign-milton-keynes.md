## Signs your Milton Keynes website is showing its age

Some sites age gracefully. Most don't. If yours was built four or five years ago and hasn't been touched since, there's a fair chance it's quietly losing you work. Here's what to look for.

One of these on its own is worth a look. Three or more, and you're almost certainly handing work to competitors with sharper, faster sites.

- It's awkward on a phone — you have to pinch, zoom and squint, and the buttons are fiddly to tap.
- Pages take more than two or three seconds to appear, especially on 4G out on the grid roads or the A5.
- The design no longer matches your branding, your prices or the services you actually offer today.
- You can't edit it yourself, or you've lost touch with whoever originally built it.
- It doesn't show up when people search for your service near Milton Keynes.
- There's no obvious next step — no clear way to call, book or send an enquiry.

## A Website Redesign in Milton Keynes Doesn't Always Mean Starting Over

Here's the part most people don't expect: a redesign often isn't a rebuild. Plenty of sites have good bones. The content is sound and the structure makes sense — it just looks dated, runs slowly and doesn't convert.

When that's the case, a refresh does the job: a modern, mobile-first design dropped onto your existing content, faster loading, tidied-up copy and photos, and clearer calls to action. A full rebuild is only worth paying for when the underlying structure is genuinely holding you back — a shop that can't take payments, say, or a site nobody can edit. A quick review tells you which camp you're in before you spend a penny.
