## Why web design in Stratford has to work harder

Few parts of London have changed as fast as Stratford. The 2012 Games left behind the Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park, East Village grew a whole new neighbourhood out of the old athletes' quarters, and Westfield Stratford City turned the area into one of the busiest retail destinations in Europe. Add the towers going up along the Lea, the crowds at Theatre Royal Stratford East and the students at the University of East London, and you have a huge, constantly refreshing pool of customers — most of them brand new to the area and with no idea you exist yet.

That is the opportunity and the problem in one. Stratford station funnels people off the Central, Jubilee, DLR, Overground and Elizabeth lines all day, and nearly all of them find local businesses the same way: they pull out a phone and search. If a plumber, café, barber or clinic near the Broadway does not show up cleanly on that small screen, the national chains inside Westfield win by default.

> In Stratford, most people meet your business on a phone screen long before they ever meet you in person.

## What a Stratford website actually has to do

A website that works here is not the prettiest one. It is the one that answers a busy, on-the-move visitor's questions in seconds. Before we design a single screen, we make sure the site can do the basics brilliantly:

- Load fast on a phone over 4G, because a good share of your visitors are searching as they walk out of Stratford station
- Put a tappable phone number, your address and today's opening hours where nobody has to hunt for them
- Show up for searches like 'barber near me' or 'dentist in Stratford', not just for your business name
- Earn trust in seconds with real photos of your place and team, and clear prices or an obvious next step
- Give someone who just moved into East Village a reason to choose you over the big name in Westfield
