## What every Clapham restaurant or cafe site needs

Whether you run a coffee shop off Abbeville Road, a bistro on The Pavement or a salon near the Old Town, the essentials are the same. A visitor should be able to do all of these without pinching, zooming or resorting to email:

- A menu that reads cleanly on a phone — real text, not a photographed PDF that no one can read and Google cannot index.
- Online booking that takes reservations while you are in the middle of service, with a tap-to-call number for walk-ins.
- Current offers up front — bottomless brunch, set lunch, happy hour or a quiet-Tuesday deal — not hidden three clicks deep.
- Photos of the real room, the plates and the atmosphere, so people know exactly what they are walking into.
- Recent reviews, because on a stretch as competitive as Venn Street they often decide who gets the booking.
- Opening hours, address and a map kept accurate for bank holidays and late nights, when Clapham is at its busiest.

## Get found when Clapham searches 'near me'

Your website and your Google Business Profile work as a pair. The profile is what appears on the map when someone searches nearby; the website is where they actually decide. Keep the two consistent — same name, same hours, same phone number — and encourage happy diners to leave reviews, because a steady flow of recent five-star ratings quietly moves you up the list. It is worth getting the basics exactly right: a profile with good photos, correct categories and quick replies to reviews often does more for a Clapham cafe than any paid advert.
