Your website probably looked the part on launch day. But Brighton is a design-literate city, full of independents, creative studios and the digital crowd around the so-called Silicon Beach, and a site that felt sharp three years ago can quietly start letting you down. If people are pulling out their phones in The Lanes or along the seafront to find you and landing on something slow or cramped, a website redesign in Brighton might be the most valuable thing you sort out this year.

The reassuring part is that modernising a tired site rarely means tearing it down and starting again. Sometimes it does; more often it does not. Here is how we think about a redesign, what tends to go wrong with older Brighton business websites, and how to tell a quick refresh from a proper rebuild before you spend anything.

## Why a dated site stands out more in Brighton

Brighton customers notice design. This is a city that runs on independent shops, cafes, creative studios and small practices, and people here are used to things looking considered. A clunky, out-of-date website does not blend into the background; it stands out next to sharper competitors in North Laine, Kemptown or along Church Road in Hove. Fair or not, visitors tend to read a tired site as a tired business.

There is a second thing that makes Brighton different. A huge slice of your traffic is people who are not local at all: day-trippers down from London, weekenders, festival and conference visitors, students at the two universities. Many are searching cold, on their phone, with no idea yet who you are. Your website is the entire first impression, and it has seconds to make it.

## The signs you are quietly losing enquiries

Most owners feel something is off before they can name it. Enquiries have thinned out. The site looks dated beside newer rivals. Or it simply falls apart on a phone, which matters more than almost anything, because most local searches in Brighton happen on mobile, often while someone is standing on a street corner deciding where to spend their money.

- It is slow to load, especially on patchy seafront and city-centre mobile signal.
- Text and buttons are fiddly on a phone, so people give up before they enquire.
- You do not appear when someone searches your service alongside Brighton, Hove or an area like Kemptown.
- The design looks years old and no longer matches the quality of your actual work.
- You cannot edit it yourself, so opening hours and prices slowly drift out of date.
- It says nothing to the summer visitors who make up so much of Brighton's trade.

## You probably do not need to start from scratch

Redesign sounds expensive, and it does not have to be. Plenty of Brighton businesses arrive braced for a full rebuild when the real trouble is three or four fixable things: a tired homepage, weak wording, an awkward mobile layout and no obvious next step for the visitor. If the bones of your [website design and build](/services/website-design) are sound, we keep what works and modernise the rest.

That is often the difference between a few hundred pounds and a few thousand, which is exactly why we look before we quote. Once we can see what you actually have, it is far easier to tell you honestly whether a light refresh will do or a rebuild makes more sense.

> A good redesign keeps what already works and fixes only what is holding you back.

## What a website redesign in Brighton involves

A redesign is more than a fresh coat of paint. We start with who you actually serve, whether that is homeowners in Hanover and Preston Park, trade clients across the city, or diners heading to your Preston Street restaurant, and we design around how they really search and decide. In practice that means faster pages, clearer wording, a layout that guides people towards an enquiry, and stronger [local SEO](/services/seo) so you surface when someone nearby types your service next to their part of town.

It also means being found in the moment that counts. A modern Brighton site should load quickly on mobile, display cleanly in Google, link tidily to your Google Business Profile, and give a visitor standing outside your door an easy way to call, book or get directions. For a lot of Brighton businesses it should also handle the summer surge gracefully, when tourist traffic can dwarf the quieter winter months.

### How long does a website redesign take?

Most of our sites go live within three to six weeks, depending on how much is changing and how quickly we get your content and photos. A light refresh can be quicker; a full rebuild with new copy and structure sits at the longer end. We always give you a clear timeline before we start.

### Do I need a full rebuild or just a refresh?

Often just a refresh. If your site is reasonably modern underneath, we can improve speed, wording and mobile layout without starting again. If it is very old or built on something fragile, a rebuild can work out cheaper over time. A free teardown is the quickest way to find out which you need.

### Will a redesign hurt my Google rankings?

Not if it is handled carefully. We map your existing pages across, set up redirects properly, and improve the things Google favours, such as speed, mobile usability and clear content. Done well, a redesign usually helps your visibility rather than harming it, though no one can honestly promise specific rankings.
