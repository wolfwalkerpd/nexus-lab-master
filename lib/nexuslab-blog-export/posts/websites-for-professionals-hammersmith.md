## What clients look for before they pick up the phone

Design taste matters, but for a professional firm the site has one job: let a visitor understand what you offer, believe you are good at it, and reach you without any friction. A few things move the needle far more than the rest:

- **Clear services in plain English** — 'self-assessment', 'conveyancing', 'R&D tax claims', 'employment advice' — not vague talk about 'bespoke solutions'.
- **Proof of who you are** — real names, faces, qualifications and how long you have been going. Anonymous firms lose to human ones.
- **Effortless contact** — a tap-to-call number that works on mobile, a short enquiry form, and your Hammersmith address on the page.
- **A sense of price or process**, so nobody fears they are walking into an open-ended bill.
- **Reassurance you are regulated and insured**, shown plainly rather than buried in the footer.

## Trust signals that matter for regulated firms

For an accountant, solicitor or consultant, the buying decision is really a trust decision, and your website either earns it in seconds or throws it away. Show your professional body membership up front — ICAEW, ACCA, CIOT for accountants; SRA regulation for solicitors — alongside genuine client testimonials and a clear, honest privacy notice. These are the details a cautious Hammersmith client scans for before they will hand over their accounts or their case.

None of this needs to be flashy. A clean layout, real photographs of your team and office, and specifics about the sectors you serve will out-convert a glossy site that says nothing concrete.
