## How people in Greenwich actually search

Greenwich is really two markets in one. On a Saturday, the streets around the Cutty Sark and Greenwich Market fill with visitors thumbing their phones for 'coffee near me', 'best Sunday roast in Greenwich' or 'things to do near the Observatory'. During the week it's residents from West Greenwich, Westcombe Park and Blackheath looking for a dentist, an electrician or a hairdresser close to home. Your website has to win both.

What they share is the phone. Most local searches happen on mobile, often while someone is standing on Greenwich High Road with somewhere else to be. If your site is slow, hides your phone number, or looks broken on a small screen, they tap back and choose the next result on the list.

- Are you open, and exactly where — a map pin near the DLR or the market saves them guessing
- Can they call, book or message you in a single tap
- Do the photos show the real place and real people, not stock images
- Does the page load before the lights change
