## Your menu is the page everyone opens first

Ask any Hackney operator which page gets the most views and it's the menu, every time. Yet so many sites bury it in a PDF that downloads to the phone, opens tiny and can't be changed without redoing the whole file. A menu should be a proper web page: readable, quick to scan, and easy to update when your chef swaps the specials.

It matters more here than most places. Hackney has one of the biggest vegan and veggie crowds in the country, and people filter hard — gluten-free, halal, dairy-free. Clear dietary labels and honest prices answer their questions instantly and save your staff a dozen DMs a day. When your menu changes weekly, a simple [care plan](/services/care-plans) means you send us the update and it's live the same day, no faff.

## Make booking a table the easy part

Instagram DMs are where a lot of Hackney bookings start, and they are a nightmare to manage on a Friday night — messages missed, tables double-booked, requests lost under comments. A booking system built into your site takes that off your plate and lets people reserve the moment they decide, which is often 11pm for a table tomorrow.

The goal is simple: everyone who wants a table can get one without waiting for you to reply. A good booking setup should:

- Take bookings 24/7, including the late-night and weekend slots when most reservations actually happen
- Ask for a deposit on larger tables to cut no-shows on busy Broadway Market Saturdays
- Plug into the system you already use — ResDiary, OpenTable, SevenRooms or a simple built-in form
- Send an automatic confirmation and reminder, so fewer people forget they booked
- Show real availability instead of a "we'll get back to you" that never converts
