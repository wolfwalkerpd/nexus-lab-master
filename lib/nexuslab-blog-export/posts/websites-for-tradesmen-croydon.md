## What websites for tradesmen in Croydon really need

Design taste matters, but for a trade the job of the site is simple: let a visitor see what you do, trust that you are good at it, and get in touch without any faff. These are the features that actually move the needle:

- **Click-to-call on every page.** On a mobile your number should be one tap away. Plenty of your Croydon enquiries will come from people who have no interest in filling in a form.
- **A short quote or callback form.** Name, postcode, phone and 'what needs doing' is enough. Ask for less and you get more enquiries, not fewer.
- **A gallery of real jobs.** Before-and-after shots of a Purley bathroom or a rewired flat in Addiscombe sell harder than any paragraph of copy.
- **Reviews front and centre.** Pull your Google and Checkatrade reviews onto the page. Croydon homeowners read them before they ever pick up the phone.
- **Your trade badges on show.** Gas Safe, NICEIC, TrustMark or guild logos tell people you are the real thing, not a chancer with a van.
- **Clear service areas.** Spell out the areas you cover so someone in Norbury or New Addington knows you will come to them.
