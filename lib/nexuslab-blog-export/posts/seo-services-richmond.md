If someone in Richmond wants a dentist, a plumber, or somewhere decent for lunch, they reach for their phone and type it into Google. Within a second they see a map with three businesses pinned to it and a few links underneath. If your business is not in that little cluster — the local map pack — you are close to invisible on the high street that matters most. Good SEO services in Richmond are about earning a place in that cluster, then holding on to it.

Richmond is a busy, competitive patch. From the shops along George Street and the Quadrant to the independents in St Margarets, Ham and East Sheen, you are rarely the only business of your kind within a mile. ## Why the map pack decides who gets the call

Most local searches happen on a phone, often when someone is already out and about — walking off Richmond Bridge, stepping out of the station, or heading up Richmond Hill after a look at the view. They are not browsing for fun. They want something now, and Google answers that intent with the map pack: three local results, complete with star ratings, opening hours and a route to the door.

Those three spots collect the lion's share of the clicks and calls. Ranking below them in the ordinary blue links still helps, but the map pack is the prize. It is also the one place a small Richmond business can genuinely out-rank a national chain, because [local SEO](/services/seo) rewards relevance and how close you are to the searcher, not the size of a marketing budget.

## What actually moves the needle

There is no clever trick here, and anyone selling you one is worth avoiding. Local ranking comes down to a handful of unglamorous things done properly and kept up. In rough order of impact, these are what matter:

- A complete, accurate Google Business Profile — the right categories, your services, correct opening hours, and real photos of your premises and team.
- A steady trickle of genuine reviews from Richmond customers, with a short reply to each one.
- Pages that clearly say what you do and where you do it — Richmond and nearby areas like St Margarets, Ham, East Sheen and Kew.
- A website that [loads fast on a phone](/services/website-design) and is easy to use with one thumb.
- The same name, address and phone number wherever your business is listed online.
- Content that answers the questions people actually type before they book or buy.

> You do not need to trick Google. You need to be the obvious, well-organised answer to what people are already searching for.

## Your Google Business Profile is your shop window

For a local business, your Google Business Profile now does the job the shop window used to. It is often the first thing a customer sees, and they judge you on it in a couple of seconds — before they ever reach your site. A thin, half-finished profile quietly loses you work every week.

Fill it out fully. Pick precise categories, list every service, add proper photos rather than a blurry logo, and keep your hours right, especially around bank holidays or when Twickenham has a match on and half of Richmond is heaving. Then ask happy customers for reviews and reply to them like a human. Whatever [your trade](/industries), this profile is usually the fastest, cheapest ranking win available to you.
