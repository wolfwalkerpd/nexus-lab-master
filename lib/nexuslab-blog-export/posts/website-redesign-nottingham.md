## Signs your Nottingham website is out of date

Most owners feel it before they can name it. The site just doesn't reflect where the business is now. These are the signals we see most often when we review sites across the city.

- It's hard to use on a phone: tiny text, fiddly buttons, and awkward scrolling, even though most of your visitors are on mobile.
- It loads slowly, especially on patchy 4G around the city centre or out towards Beeston and Arnold.
- The design looks dated next to newer competitors in your trade.
- You can't edit it yourself, so simple changes mean emailing whoever built it, if they still reply.
- Your opening hours, prices or services are wrong because updating them is a hassle.
- It doesn't show up when people search for what you do in Nottingham.

## How a dated site loses you Nottingham customers

Nottingham is a competitive, connected city. Every position you drop on the results page is another customer handed to the business above you.

## A website redesign in Nottingham doesn't always mean starting over

This is where a lot of owners hesitate, picturing months of work and a frightening invoice. It rarely goes that way. A good [website redesign](/services/website-design) starts by keeping what already works, such as your brand, your strongest content and the pages that bring in enquiries, then fixing what doesn't.

Sometimes that means a full rebuild on modern foundations. Just as often it's a lighter refresh: a cleaner design, faster loading, a clearer structure and copy that actually sounds like you. And once it's live, a simple monthly [care plan](/services/care-plans) keeps it updated, backed up and secure, so it never drifts back into feeling out of date.
