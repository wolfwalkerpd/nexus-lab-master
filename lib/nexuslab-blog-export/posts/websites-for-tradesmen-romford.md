## Why Romford keeps trades busy

Romford sits in the London Borough of Havering, right on the Essex border, and its housing mix keeps plumbers, sparkies and builders in steady demand. There are the Victorian and Edwardian terraces near the town centre and Raphael Park, the sought-after period homes of the Gidea Park conservation area, interwar semis across Rise Park and Ardleigh Green, big post-war estates in Harold Hill and Collier Row, and newer developments like Beam Park adding thousands of homes. Older properties need repairs and rewires; newer ones need fit-outs and extensions.

Add the Elizabeth line at Romford station, which has made the town a magnet for commuters buying and doing up homes, plus a healthy number of landlords and rented properties, and there is plenty of work to go round. The catch is simple: it only comes to you if customers can find you online and trust what they see in the first few seconds. That is where most local trades leave money on the table.

> Customers can't judge your wiring from a photo — they judge whether they can trust you from your website.

## What good websites for tradesmen in Romford include

None of this is flashy. It is the unglamorous plumbing of a site that books work, and it is exactly what we build into every project.

- A quote or enquiry form that asks for the job, the postcode and a photo — so you can price sensibly before you drive out to RM3.
- Click-to-call buttons on every page, because most people find you on a phone and want to ring there and then, not fill in ten fields.
- A before-and-after gallery of real jobs — a re-tiled bathroom in Emerson Park, a consumer unit swap in Rush Green — that shows the standard of your work.
- Genuine reviews sitting right next to your call to action, ideally pulled straight from your Google profile.
- Clear service pages (boiler repairs, rewires, extensions) and the areas you cover, so both customers and Google know exactly what you do and where.
- Fast loading and a mobile-first layout, because a site that stalls on 4G loses the job before it has even opened.

## Turning a search into a booked job

A smart-looking site is worth nothing if it never surfaces when someone searches, and it is worth just as little if it looks great but gives no obvious next step. The two have to work together: get found, then make it effortless to contact you.

Around Romford, getting found means a properly set-up and verified Google Business Profile, your name, address and phone number kept consistent everywhere, and pages that name the places you actually work — Harold Wood, Chadwell Heath, Hornchurch, Upminster, Rainham — rather than a vague "Essex and East London". When a customer in Collier Row searches, Google should have an easy reason to show your business over a national lead-generation site that will only sell your details on to three of your competitors.
