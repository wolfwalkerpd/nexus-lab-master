## The Brixton map pack, explained

The map pack is that box of three local businesses Google drops near the top of the page, each with a star rating, a pin and a tap-to-call button. For anything with local intent, it's the most valuable space on the screen.

Those three slots are chosen mainly on relevance (how well you match the search), proximity (how close you are to the person searching) and prominence (how well known and trusted your business looks online). You can't move your premises closer to everyone, but relevance and prominence are firmly within your control.

> In a place as busy as Brixton, the map pack is prime real estate — three results, and most taps never go any lower.

## What actually moves the needle

Ignore anyone promising secret tricks. Local ranking comes down to a handful of unglamorous things done consistently:

- **Your Google Business Profile** — claimed, fully filled in, with the right categories, accurate hours, real photos of the place and the odd post. This is the single biggest lever for the map pack.
- **Genuine reviews** — a steady stream from real local customers, with you replying to them. A business with plenty of recent reviews will usually rank above one with a handful.
- **Consistent details** — your name, address and phone number identical everywhere they appear, from your own site to local directories.
- **On-page local content** — pages that name the areas you serve, from Brixton and Stockwell to Herne Hill and Clapham, and answer the questions people actually ask.
- **A fast, mobile-friendly site** — most local searches happen on a phone, and a sluggish site quietly costs you both rankings and bookings.
- **Local mentions** — being featured by Brixton blogs, community groups and neighbourhood press tells Google you genuinely belong here.
