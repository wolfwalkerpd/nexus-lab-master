Your website was probably fine when it launched. But Glasgow moves fast, and a site that looked sharp three or four years ago can quietly start working against you. ## Signs your Glasgow website is due a redesign

Most owners sense something is off before they can name it. Enquiries have gone quiet. The site feels dated next to newer competitors on Sauchiehall Street or in Finnieston. Or it simply looks wrong on a phone, which matters more than almost anything else, because most local searches in Glasgow happen on mobile, often while someone is standing on the pavement deciding where to spend their money.

If two or three of those sound familiar, you are not imagining it. A dated site does not only look tired; it costs you the enquiries you never hear about, because people move on before they ever pick up the phone.

- It is slow to load, especially on 4G around the city centre and West End.
- Text and buttons are fiddly on a phone, so people give up before they enquire.
- You do not show up when people search for your service alongside Glasgow, or an area like Shawlands or Dennistoun.
- The design looks a decade old and no longer matches how good your actual work is.
- You cannot update it yourself, so opening hours and prices quietly drift out of date.

## A redesign rarely means starting from scratch

The word redesign sounds expensive, and it does not have to be. Plenty of Glasgow businesses come to us braced for a full rebuild when the real problem is three or four fixable things: a tired homepage, weak wording, a clumsy mobile layout and no clear next step for the visitor. If the bones of your [website design and build](/services/website-design) are sound, we keep what works and modernise the rest.

That is usually the difference between a few hundred pounds and a few thousand, which is why we start by looking rather than quoting. Once we can actually see what you have, it is far easier to tell you honestly whether you need a light refresh or a proper rebuild.

> A good redesign keeps what is already working and fixes only what is holding you back.

## What a website redesign in Glasgow actually involves

A redesign is more than a fresh coat of paint. We start by understanding who you serve, whether that is homeowners in Shawlands and the Southside, trade clients across Lanarkshire, or diners heading to your Finnieston restaurant, and we design around how they really search and decide. In practice that means faster pages, clearer wording, a layout that leads to an enquiry, and stronger [local SEO](/services/seo) so you appear when someone nearby types your service alongside their part of the city.

It also means being found in the moment that counts. A modern Glasgow site should load quickly on 4G, display correctly on Google, link cleanly to your Google Business Profile, and give someone standing outside your door on a wet Tuesday an easy way to call, book or get directions. Those small details add up to more real enquiries over a year than any single flashy feature.

### How long does a website redesign take?

Most of our sites go live within three to six weeks, depending on how much is changing and how quickly we get your content and photos. A light refresh can be quicker; a full rebuild with new copy and structure sits at the longer end. We give you a clear timeline before we start.

### Do I need a full rebuild or just a refresh?

Often just a refresh. If your site is reasonably modern underneath, we can improve speed, wording and mobile layout without starting again. If it is very old or built on something fragile, a rebuild can work out cheaper in the long run. A free teardown is the quickest way to find out which you need.

### Will a redesign hurt my Google rankings?

Not if it is done carefully. We keep your existing pages mapped across, set up redirects properly, and improve the things Google favours, such as speed, mobile usability and clear content. Done well, a redesign usually helps your visibility rather than harming it, though no one can honestly promise specific rankings.
