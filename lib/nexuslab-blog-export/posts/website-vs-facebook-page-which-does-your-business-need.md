If you run a small business in the UK, you have probably asked yourself the same thing more than once: website vs Facebook page, which does your business need? It is a fair question. A Facebook page is free, quick to set up, and where plenty of your customers already spend their evenings. A website costs money and takes a little planning. So is it worth having both, or will one of them do the job on its own?

The honest answer is that they do two different jobs. One helps people notice you; the other helps them trust you and take action. Which one matters most depends on how customers find you and what you are asking them to do next. This guide walks you through it in plain English — no jargon, no pressure — so you can put your time and money where they actually count.

## What a Facebook page does well

A Facebook page earns its place. It is free, it takes minutes to create, and it puts you in front of people who are already scrolling. For a café posting the day's specials, a barber showing off fresh cuts, or a florist sharing this week's arrangements, it is a brilliant way to stay visible and keep the conversation going. Customers can message you, leave reviews, and share your posts with friends — which is word of mouth doing its work at speed.

If your business runs on regular updates and a loyal local following, a Facebook page will pull its weight. The trouble starts the moment someone wants more than a quick scroll — when they are ready to compare you, check your prices, or actually book.

## Where a Facebook page holds you back

The first problem is ownership. You do not own your Facebook page — Facebook does. The rules, the reach and the layout are theirs to change whenever they choose, and you can wake up one morning to find far fewer people are seeing your posts unless you pay to boost them. You are also sitting right next to your competitors, other people's adverts, and every distraction the feed can throw at a customer.

The second problem is Google. When someone types 'emergency plumber Bristol' or 'wedding photographer near me', it is proper websites that fill the results, not Facebook pages. If you rely on a page alone, you are close to invisible to the people who are actively looking to spend money right now — and those are the customers you most want to reach.

> A Facebook page rents you an audience. A website means you own the shop.

## What a website does that a Facebook page can't

A website is the one place online that is completely yours. It works around the clock, answers the questions your customers actually have, and it is built to be found on Google. Here is what it gives you that a page never will:

- You own it outright. Your site, your domain and your data — with no landlord who can change the rules or quietly switch off your reach.
- You get found on Google. A well-built site with sensible [local SEO](/services/seo) can appear exactly when people search for what you sell.
- You look the part. A tidy, professional site tells a first-time visitor you are a real, settled business worth trusting with their money.
- You control the journey. Booking forms, clear prices, service pages and obvious next steps guide people towards enquiring or buying.
- You keep their full attention. No adverts, no rival posts, no bottomless feed — just your business and the customer.

## Website vs Facebook page: which does your business need?

Here is the honest steer. If you are testing an idea this weekend, have next to no budget, and simply want to post updates to a local crowd, start with a Facebook page. It costs nothing and gets you moving today.

But the moment you want to be found on Google, take bookings, look credible to complete strangers, or stop depending on someone else's platform, you need a website. For most established local businesses — trades, clinics, salons, restaurants and professional services — that moment has already arrived. A [professional website](/services/website-design) becomes the foundation, and the Facebook page becomes one of several ways people arrive at it.

## The smart order: build the foundation, then feed it

You do not have to pick one forever. The businesses that get this right use both, in the right order. Your website is home base — where your prices, services and booking sit, and where Google sends people who are searching. Your Facebook page is a shop window on a busy street, there to catch attention and point people back home.

So keep the page for daily posts, offers and friendly chat, and send every visitor to your site to actually book, buy or enquire. And whoever ends up looking after it, make sure the site stays fast, updated and secure — that is exactly what a simple [care plan](/services/care-plans) is for, so nothing quietly breaks in the background while you are busy running the business.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I just use a Facebook page instead of a website?

You can, and for a brand-new side project it is a sensible place to start. But a Facebook page rarely shows up on Google, you do not own it, and Facebook can change your reach at any time. Once you want to be found by people searching for what you sell, a website becomes the safer home for your business.

### Do I still need a Facebook page if I have a website?

Often, yes — they work best together. Your website is where people book, buy and enquire, and where Google sends searchers. Your Facebook page is a friendly way to stay visible, post updates and chat with regulars, all pointing back to your site.
