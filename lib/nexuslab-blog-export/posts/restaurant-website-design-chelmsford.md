## The features every hospitality site in Chelmsford needs

Whether you run a bistro on Baddow Road, a coffee shop near the Cathedral or a salon off Duke Street, the essentials are the same. Get these right and your website does the selling while you focus on the room.

None of this needs to be complicated. It needs to be fast, honest and easy to update — the same way you would run the front of house.

- A menu written as real text on the page, not a PDF or a photo — so both diners and Google can read it, and you can change a price in seconds
- Online booking or a big click-to-call button that works with a single thumb
- Opening hours that are always current, including the bank holiday and event-day exceptions people check first
- Photos of your actual food, drinks and room — never stock images of someone else's plate
- Your address, a map and a note on parking, which is a very real Chelmsford question near the city centre
- A clear space for offers: set lunch, happy hour, student nights or a weekend brunch deal
