If you run a café in Leeds, a plumbing round in Bristol or a hair salon in Cardiff, most of your next customers will find you the same way. They type what they need into Google and pick from the first few results. Learning how to get your business on Google is one of the highest-value things a small business can do, and the best part is that most of it is free and well within your reach.

This guide walks through it in plain English — no jargon, no expensive tools. We'll cover the free listing that puts you on the map, the website that helps you rank, and the everyday habits that keep you visible. And if you'd rather hand the whole thing over, we'll be honest about that too.

## First, understand where you can show up

When someone searches for a local service, Google shows businesses in two main free spots. There's the map pack — the little map near the top with three businesses pinned to it — and there are the ordinary blue-link results below it. Paid ads sit above both, but you don't have to buy your way in to be seen.

Most local searches happen on a phone, often with the words "near me" attached. So the person looking for you is usually nearby, ready to call or visit, and deciding in seconds. Your job is to appear in those free spots and give them an easy reason to choose you.

> You don't need to pay Google to appear on Google — the listings that earn the most trust are the free ones.

## How to get your business on Google for free

The single most important step is a Google Business Profile (you may remember it as Google My Business). It's free, it powers that map pack, and it's the fastest way to appear at all. Go to google.com/business, search for your business, then either claim the existing listing or create a new one. Google will verify that you're really you — usually by post, phone or a short video.

Once you're in, the difference between invisible and chosen is how completely you fill it out. Give Google — and your customers — everything they need:

- Your exact business name, address and phone number, written identically everywhere online
- The most accurate categories you can pick (for example "emergency plumber", not just "plumber")
- Real photos of your premises, team and finished work, rather than stock images
- Opening hours, including bank holidays and seasonal changes
- A short, honest description of what you do and the areas you cover
- Your key products or services, with prices where it helps people decide

> A half-finished profile is the most common reason a good business stays invisible. Complete listings get chosen far more often.

## Give Google a proper website to trust

Your profile gets you onto the map. A website gets you into the blue-link results and gives Google a reason to trust you over time. It doesn't need to be big — a handful of clear, well-written pages beats a bloated site every time. Aim for a simple homepage, a page for each main service, a page about the areas you cover, and contact details that match your Business Profile word for word.

Speed and mobile matter more than looks. If a page is slow or fiddly to read on a phone, people leave before they've read a word. This is exactly what good [website design](/services/website-design) and solid [local SEO](/services/seo) are for: pages that load fast, read well and tell Google clearly what you do and where you do it.

## Reviews, posts and staying visible

Getting on Google is step one. Staying near the top is the ongoing part, and it's mostly about steady habits rather than clever tricks. Reviews are the biggest lever a small business has, so ask every happy customer for one and reply to each review you receive, good or bad. It shows Google you're active and shows customers you care.

Beyond that, keep your information accurate whenever anything changes, post the occasional update or offer, and pick up a few local mentions — your trade body, a chamber of commerce, a supplier's website. None of it is dramatic. Consistency over months is what quietly moves you up, and there are no shortcuts or guaranteed rankings, whatever anyone tells you.

### How do I get my business on Google for free?

Create a **Google Business Profile** at google.com/business, verify it when Google asks, then fill it in fully — name, address, phone, categories, hours, photos and services. That free listing is what puts you in the map results, and it's the single most effective free step you can take.
