These people are not browsing for fun. They want an answer in seconds, and if your site makes them pinch, zoom and wait, they have already tapped back to Google and picked the restaurant below you.

A website built around these habits — fast, mobile-first, with the important things one tap away — quietly outperforms a prettier site that ignores them.

- Checking your menu and prices before they commit — usually the very first thing they look for.

## Your menu is the page that sells

Ask most Woking restaurateurs for their menu online and you get a PDF that was scanned three years ago. On a phone it opens as a tiny, blurry document nobody can read without zooming, and Google cannot read it at all. Your menu is the single most visited page you have, so it deserves to be a proper, fast-loading web page — clear headings, readable prices, dietary markers and a photo or two of the dishes people actually order.

When we handle [restaurant and cafe website design](/services/website-design), the menu is where we start. It updates in minutes from a simple admin panel, so when the specials change or a supplier puts prices up, you are not waiting on a developer to make the edit.

> Your menu is not an attachment. It is the most persuasive page on your website.

## Make booking a table effortless

Every tap between 'I would like to eat here' and 'table booked' is a chance to lose someone. The best restaurant sites make it feel like one motion: a clear book-a-table button that follows the visitor down the page, a phone number that dials with a single tap on mobile, and, if you take reservations online, a booking tool that works cleanly on a small screen.

If you run a busy Friday service, an online booking that syncs to your table plan saves your team from a phone that never stops ringing. If you are a small cafe or a walk-in bistro, you may not need bookings at all — clear hours, a map and a tap-to-call number do more. The point is to remove friction, not pile on features you will never use.

## Let reviews and real photos do the talking

Woking diners trust other Woking diners. A wall of genuine, recent reviews and real photographs of your food, your room and your team will convince a first-timer faster than any amount of clever copy. Stock images of generic pasta do the opposite — they make people wonder what you are hiding.

Ask your happy regulars to leave a Google review, then feature the best ones where new visitors see them first — near your menu and your booking button, not buried on a page of their own. We build galleries around photos of your actual dishes, so the site feels like the place people will walk into.
