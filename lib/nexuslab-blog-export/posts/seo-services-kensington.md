Two things follow from this. First, your customers research online and judge quickly, so a slow or dated website loses them in seconds. Second, most of that searching happens on a phone, often with 'near me' attached, whether someone is walking through Kensington Gardens or coming out of Gloucester Road station. If your business does not show up clearly on mobile, you are invisible to a lot of ready-to-buy people.

## The Google map pack: three spots worth fighting for

When someone searches for a service near them, Google usually shows a small map with three business listings above the normal blue links. That is the local map pack, and for a Kensington business it is prime real estate. Those three spots take a large share of the clicks and calls because they sit right at the top, with reviews, opening hours and a route already attached.

Getting into the pack is mostly about your Google Business Profile and the signals around it. Google is trying to answer one question: is this business relevant, close and trusted enough to recommend? You influence that answer with a few concrete things.

- A complete, accurate Google Business Profile with the correct category, address, hours, photos and services.
- The same name, address and phone number wherever your business appears online.
- A steady flow of genuine reviews, and replies to them, rather than a rush of ten and then silence.
- A website that clearly states what you do and where you do it, so Google can match you to local searches.
- Mentions and links from other local sources, from directories to nearby organisations.

## Quick wins most Kensington websites are missing

You do not need a big budget to make progress. A lot of local sites leave easy ground uncovered, and closing those gaps often moves you up faster than any clever tactic. If you do nothing else, start here.

- Give each service its own page, and where it makes sense, the areas you cover too, such as South Kensington, Notting Hill or Holland Park, instead of one vague services page.
- Put your address and a map on the site, matched exactly to your Google profile.
- Ask happy customers for a review while they are still in the room or on the phone.
- Compress your images and remove anything that slows your pages down, especially on mobile.
- Write in plain terms about what you do, so both people and Google can understand it at a glance.
