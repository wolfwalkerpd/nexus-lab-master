## How Birmingham customers actually find you

Birmingham is a big, spread-out city, and people search accordingly. Someone in Moseley looking for a plumber, a shopper in the Bullring wanting a nearby lunch spot, a homeowner in Sutton Coldfield after a builder — most of them are searching on a mobile, often with the word "near me" or a district name attached. If your site is slow, hard to read on a phone, or missing from Google Maps, you're invisible at the exact moment someone's ready to buy.

That's why local matters so much. A website built for a Birmingham business should make it obvious where you are, which areas you cover — Digbeth, Edgbaston, Erdington, Kings Heath — and how to reach you in one tap. Pair that with a properly set-up Google Business Profile and you start showing up when it counts.

- Most local searches happen on a mobile, so the phone experience comes first, not last
- "Near me" and district-name searches reward sites that name their real service areas
- A clear map, phone number and opening hours remove friction for ready-to-buy customers
- Google Business Profile and your website work together to lift your local visibility
