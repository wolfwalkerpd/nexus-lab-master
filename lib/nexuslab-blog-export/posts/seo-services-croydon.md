## The Google map pack is the real prize

For anything with a location or a service area, the block of three businesses shown on the little map — the 'map pack' — sits above almost everything else on the page. It displays your name, star rating, opening hours and a button to call or get directions in one tap. For someone standing on North End deciding where to go, that box often is the search; they may never scroll past it.

The map pack is powered by your Google Business Profile, not your website on its own. If that profile is thin, out of date, or missing categories and photos, you can have a beautiful site and still be invisible where it matters most. Claiming and finishing your profile is usually the single highest-value fix we find when we review a Croydon business.

## What actually moves the needle

Local SEO has a lot of moving parts, but a short list does most of the heavy lifting. Get these right before you worry about anything clever:

Notice what is not on that list: buying dodgy links, stuffing your pages with 'Croydon' twenty times, or paying for guaranteed rankings. Anyone promising you a fixed position on Google is guessing at best, and risking a penalty at worst.

- **A complete Google Business Profile** — correct categories, service areas, opening hours and real photos of your work, premises or team.
- **Consistent name, address and phone number** across every listing. If your details read differently on Google, Facebook and Yell, it quietly chips away at trust.
- **Genuine reviews, answered promptly.** A steady trickle of honest reviews from Croydon customers does more than any keyword trick — and a quick, human reply shows you are a real, active business.
- **Local pages that actually say something** — a page about the areas you cover, written for people in Purley or Addiscombe rather than stuffed with town names.
- **A fast, mobile-friendly website**, because most local searches happen on a phone and a slow page loses the click before it even loads.
