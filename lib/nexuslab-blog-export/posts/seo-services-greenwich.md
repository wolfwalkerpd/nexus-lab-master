## The map pack is the new shop window

When someone in Greenwich searches for what you sell, Google usually replies in two parts. First comes the local map pack, a small map with three businesses pinned to it and their star ratings. Below that sits the ordinary list of links. On a phone the map pack fills most of the screen, and most local searches happen on a phone, so those three spots collect the lion's share of the calls and clicks. Everyone else is left fighting over the scroll.

Getting into that pack is a different job from ranking a normal web page. It leans heavily on your Google Business Profile, your reviews and how close you are to the person searching. You can't move your unit off Creek Road or Greenwich High Road, but you can control almost everything else that decides whether you appear.

## Get your Google Business Profile working hard

For most Greenwich businesses this is where the fastest wins live, and it costs nothing. Claim and verify your profile, then treat it as a living page rather than a form you filled in once and forgot. Reviews matter most of all here: they nudge you up the rankings and are often the deciding factor when someone is choosing between you and the business pinned just below.

- Choose the most accurate primary category, then add relevant secondary ones.
- Write a short, honest description that mentions Greenwich and what you genuinely do.
- Add real photos of your premises, team and work, not stock images.
- Keep your opening hours accurate, especially around bank holidays and big events at the O2.
- Ask happy customers for reviews, and reply to every one, good or bad.
