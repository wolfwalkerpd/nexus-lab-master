## Wimbledon is really two search markets

Search behaviour here splits neatly down the middle. Up in Wimbledon Village, the customers browsing boutiques, salons, estate agents and private clinics tend to research carefully and expect a polished, credible business. Down by the station and the Centre Court shopping centre, and out through South Wimbledon, Colliers Wood and Raynes Park, searches are quicker and more practical - someone wants a job done and wants it soon.

Whichever end you sit at, the searches are hyper-local. People rarely type 'dentist' or 'electrician' on their own. They type 'dentist Wimbledon Village', 'electrician SW19' or 'personal trainer near Wimbledon Common', usually on a mobile and often on the move between the tram, the District line and the mainline platforms. If your website and Google Business Profile do not make it obvious which of those areas you serve, you drop out of the running before the search has really begun.

## The map pack decides who gets the call

The map pack is the block of three businesses Google drops at the top of almost every local search. It is built from Google Business Profiles rather than websites, and for a business in SW19 it is the most valuable slice of screen there is. The paid listings sit above it, but the map pack is the first thing most people actually trust and tap.

Google chooses those three using three broad ingredients: relevance (how closely you match what was typed), distance (how near you are to the person searching) and prominence (how established and well-reviewed you look across the web). You cannot pick up your premises and move it closer to everyone in Merton, but relevance and prominence are far more in your hands than most owners realise - and that is where the work pays off.

> Three slots on the map, a whole town searching - that is what winning local Wimbledon comes down to.

## What actually moves your ranking in SW19

There is no single trick to any of this. Ranking well across Wimbledon comes from a handful of unglamorous things done properly and kept up:

Do these consistently and you pull ahead of competitors who set up a profile once and never looked at it again. If you would rather not juggle it yourself, our [local SEO service](/services/seo) handles the lot.

- Claim and finish your Google Business Profile properly: the correct categories, real photos of your premises or work, accurate opening hours and the odd post. This is the ground the map pack is built on.
- Earn reviews steadily and reply to every one. A handful of honest reviews each month, with your responses underneath, counts for far more than a sudden pile that then dries up.
- Keep your name, address and phone number identical everywhere you appear, from your own site to Google, Yell and Facebook. Small mismatches quietly cost you trust with Google.
- Give each service and each area its own page - dentistry marketed to the Village reads differently to the same service pitched to families in Raynes Park or Colliers Wood.
- Write like a local: name SW19, the Broadway, Wimbledon Common and the streets you genuinely serve, rather than a vague 'we cover South West London'.
- Pick up a few real local links - a Merton business directory, a neighbouring shop, a supplier, or a school or club you sponsor. Relevance beats volume every time.
