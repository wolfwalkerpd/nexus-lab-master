If your website was built a few years ago and hasn't been touched since, you're not alone. Plenty of good businesses across Edinburgh are running on sites that looked fine at launch but now load slowly, break on a phone and quietly turn visitors away. The good news is that a **website redesign in Edinburgh** rarely means throwing everything out and starting from zero.

This is a practical look at what actually goes wrong with an ageing site, how to tell a tired one from a broken one and what a redesign genuinely changes for a local business. Whether you're a dentist off Morningside Road, a builder covering the EH postcodes or a cafe in Leith, the same handful of problems tend to cost you the same enquiries.

## How to tell your Edinburgh site is holding you back

A dated website doesn't announce itself. It just quietly underperforms while you get on with running the business. But the signs are consistent, and most owners recognise a few of them straight away.

Edinburgh customers search on the move, walking down Princes Street, sitting on the tram or checking their phone in a queue on the Royal Mile. If your site makes them pinch and zoom, or takes five seconds to load on a patchy signal, they're gone before they've read a word. Most local searches now happen on mobile, so a site that only looks right on a desktop is losing people every day.

- It loads slowly, especially on a phone or a weak mobile signal
- Text and buttons are tiny on mobile, or the layout spills off the screen
- You can't update it yourself, so simple changes wait weeks or cost a fee
- The design looks a decade old next to your competitors
- It doesn't show up when people search for your service near you
- Your opening hours, prices or team page are quietly out of date

## Why a redesign beats limping along

A website is often the first impression a customer gets, well before they phone or walk in. In a city where people compare three or four local firms in a couple of minutes, a slow or awkward site plants a doubt: if the website is this fiddly, what's the rest of the business like?

A good redesign fixes that impression and the mechanics underneath it. Faster pages, a layout that works thumb-first on mobile, clear next steps, and content written so both people and Google understand what you do and where you do it. That last part matters in Edinburgh, where competition for terms like your trade plus a neighbourhood name can be fierce. Pairing a redesign with proper [local SEO](/services/seo) is usually where the real gains show up.

It also future-proofs the basics. Every site we build includes a simple admin panel so you can change your own hours, prices and photos without ringing anyone, and you keep full ownership of the site, domain and data. No lock-in, no held-hostage logins.

## You might not need a full rebuild

Here's the part most agencies won't lead with: plenty of Edinburgh sites don't need tearing down. If the foundations are sound, a redesign can be surgical rather than total.

Sometimes the structure is fine and it's the design, speed and copy that let it down. Sometimes the branding still works and you just need mobile fixed, a faster host and a clearer path to enquiry. We'll always tell you when a tidy-up will do the job, because a smaller, honest piece of work that gets results beats an expensive rebuild you didn't need. If you do want the full picture of what's involved, our [website design](/services/website-design) page walks through how we approach a build.

- Keep it: solid structure, working branding, content that's mostly right
- Redesign it: dated look, poor mobile experience, slow pages, weak enquiry path
- Rebuild it: broken on mobile, insecure, impossible to update, no clear structure
