## What the best websites for tradesmen in Slough do differently

A website that wins work isn't a digital brochure you build once and forget. It is a tool with a job to do: turn a stranger's search into a booked call. The details that matter are boring and practical, not decorative.

Get these right and everything else follows. Get them wrong and even a smart-looking site will leak jobs day after day.

- Click-to-call buttons that work with one thumb — most people searching for a trade are on a mobile, often in a hurry.
- A short quote form that asks only what you need: name, postcode, the job, and the best time to call.
- A photo gallery of real work — before-and-afters, finished bathrooms, tidy consumer units, an extension you built out in Langley.
- Genuine Google reviews pulled straight through, with the customer's area shown so they read as local.
- Clear service areas and trades, so a Burnham homeowner knows at a glance that you cover them.
- Fast loading and an honest, mobile-first layout — a slow site loses the job before you have said a word.
