## What SEO Services in Stratford Actually Involve

Local SEO is simply the work that helps nearby customers find you when they search. In Stratford that splits into two halves. The first is your Google Business Profile — the listing that decides whether you appear on the map, in Google Maps and in the 'near me' results people tap on their phones. The second is your website: the pages Google reads to understand what you do, who you serve and where.

Get both pulling in the same direction and you start showing up for searches like 'dentist E15', 'emergency electrician Stratford' or 'barber near Westfield'. Miss one and you leave easy work on the table for a competitor down the road on Angel Lane.

## Why the Map Pack Matters More Than Your Homepage

When you search for a local service, Google usually shows three businesses in a box with a small map above the ordinary blue links. That box is the map pack, and in a place as busy as Stratford it does most of the heavy lifting. Think about how people actually search here: coming off the Central line, walking through Stratford station's ticket hall, or standing in a queue at Westfield with a phone in hand. They want somewhere close, open now and well reviewed — and they rarely scroll past those first three results.

Ranking in that box comes down to three things Google weighs: relevance, distance and prominence. You can't move your shopfront, but you can influence the other two — how clearly your profile matches the search, and how trusted your business looks. That is where good [local SEO](/services/seo) earns its keep.

## What Actually Moves the Needle

There's a long list of things you could do, but a short list of things that matter. For most Stratford businesses, the wins come from getting these right:

- A complete Google Business Profile — correct category, opening hours, services, photos of your actual premises, and posts kept up to date.
- Consistent details everywhere — your name, address and phone number written identically on your site, your profile and any directories. Mismatches confuse Google.
- Real reviews, gathered steadily, with a genuine reply to each one. A slow trickle beats a sudden burst, which can look bought.
- A fast, mobile-first website, because most local searches in Stratford happen on a phone, often on patchy signal near the station. A [website that loads quickly](/services/website-design) stops people bouncing back to the results.
- Dedicated pages for each service and area you cover — Stratford, Maryland, Forest Gate, Leyton, Bow — rather than one thin page trying to do everything.
- Local links and mentions — a Newham directory, a nearby supplier, the school newsletter you sponsored. Each one tells Google you're genuinely part of the area.

## Standing Out Next to Westfield and the Big Chains

It's easy to feel outgunned when a giant shopping centre sits on your doorstep and every national chain has a listing. But the chains are often the reason you can win. Their listings are generic, their reviews are pooled across hundreds of branches, and they rarely tailor anything to Stratford specifically. A local business that names its neighbourhood, answers real questions and shows photos of the actual E15 premises looks far more relevant to someone searching nearby.

The trick is to be specific about who you are and who you serve. 'Family dentist near Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park' or 'same-day boiler repair across E15 and E20' will always beat a vague 'quality service, competitive prices'. Specific wins because it matches the way real people type and speak.

## A Sensible Order to Start

If you're doing this yourself, tackle it in the order that pays back fastest. Claim and complete your Google Business Profile first — it's free and it moves quickly. Next, tidy your website so it loads fast on mobile and has a clear page for each service. Then build content and reviews steadily over the following weeks. Finally, chase a few good local links.
