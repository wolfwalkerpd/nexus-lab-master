## Web design in Nottingham: what your site is really for

A website earns its keep when it turns a stranger's search into a phone call, a booking or a filled-in form. For most local firms here, the people finding you are only a few miles away — someone in Beeston hunting for an emergency plumber, a couple in Mapperley choosing a wedding photographer, a landlord near the Lace Market who needs an electrician this week. They are not weighing you against a national brand. They are weighing you against the other two or three Nottingham businesses that came up in the same search.

So the job of your site is refreshingly simple: load quickly, look like you can be trusted, and make the next step obvious. Miss any one of those and people tap straight back to Google and try the next result. We design around that single goal — more local enquiries — rather than clever features nobody ever uses. You can see the [kind of sites we design](/services/website-design) for local trades, clinics and shops across the city.

## How people in Nottingham actually find you

Most local searches happen on a mobile, often on the move — on the tram into town, waiting outside the Victoria Centre, or sat in a café near the Old Market Square. With two big universities and a young, phone-first population, that mobile bias is stronger here than in a lot of places. It changes what your site has to get right.

Getting found is half web design and half [local SEO](/services/seo), and the two work best when they are planned together from the very start rather than bolted on afterwards.

- Speed beats polish. A page that takes five seconds to load on 4G near the Broad Marsh loses the click before anyone reads a word.
- Your Google Business Profile does much of the heavy lifting for 'near me' searches, so it needs to match your site and stay up to date.
- Reviews and a clear local address tell someone in Wollaton or West Bridgford that you are genuinely nearby, not a call centre two counties away.
- Thumbs, not mouse pointers — buttons and phone numbers have to be easy to tap one-handed.
