## Is it time for a website redesign in Cardiff?

Some sites fail all at once, but most just drift. The design that looked sharp at launch starts to feel dated, the pages load a beat too slowly, and enquiries quietly tail off without any single thing going obviously wrong. If a few of the signs below sound familiar, it's worth taking a proper look.

None of these mean your business has a problem. They mean your website has stopped keeping up with it, and that is a straightforward thing to put right.

- It was built five or more years ago, and it shows: narrow layouts, small text and styling that feels of its time.
- It's slow, especially on mobile data, which matters when so many people search on their phone on the move.
- It's awkward on a phone: pinch-to-zoom, fiddly buttons and forms that are a chore to fill in.
- You can't update it yourself, so your opening hours, prices or photos are out of date.
- It no longer matches your brand, your prices or the way you actually work today.

## You may not need a full rebuild

Here's the part people don't expect: plenty of sites don't need tearing down to the studs. If the underlying structure is sound and only the look, speed and words have aged, a focused refresh can do the job for a fraction of the cost and disruption of a full rebuild.

Sometimes, though, the foundations really are the problem: a platform that fights every change, or a structure that was never built for how your business works now. A short teardown is how we tell the difference, so you're not paying for a [full redesign](/services/website-design) you don't need, or patching a site that's beyond saving.

- A cleaner, modern design applied to your existing pages.
- Faster loading through compressed images and better hosting.
- Rewritten, plain-English copy that says what you do and where you do it.
- A proper mobile layout with clear, obvious calls to action.

## Keeping your new site working

A redesign isn't a one-off event you can then forget. Software needs updating, backups need running, and the small changes pile up: a new price, a seasonal offer, a fresh set of photos from a recent job. Left alone, even a brand-new site slowly slides back towards the state that made you redesign in the first place.

That's what a [care plan](/services/care-plans) is for. For a lot of Cardiff businesses, especially those serving customers in both Welsh and English, it's the difference between a site that keeps earning its keep and one that quietly rots.
