Ask ten agencies what does SEO cost for a small business in the UK and you will get ten different numbers, and a lot of shrugging. It genuinely depends on your trade, your town and the state of your website, but that is no excuse for keeping you in the dark. So here are real, honest ranges, what pushes the price up or down, and how to make sure every pound pulls its weight.

SEO is simply the work of helping people find you on Google when they search for what you sell: a new patient looking for a dentist, someone hunting an emergency plumber at 9pm, a couple comparing wedding venues. Done well, it brings in enquiries for years. Done cheaply, it can quietly drain your budget. Knowing the going rate is how you tell the two apart.

## What does SEO cost for a UK small business?

Most UK small businesses pay for SEO in one of two ways: a monthly retainer for ongoing work, or a one-off project to fix and improve things. As a rough guide to what you will typically see:

For most local shops, trades and practices, sensible spending sits somewhere between £300 and £1,200 a month, or a well-scoped one-off you can budget for in advance. Our own [SEO work](/services/seo) is quoted per project once we have actually looked at your site, never a number plucked from thin air.

- Freelancers and solo consultants: often £250–£600 a month, or a few hundred pounds for a one-off local setup.
- Small, UK-based agencies: usually £500–£1,500 a month for ongoing local SEO.
- One-off projects, like a technical fix or a batch of location pages: commonly £500–£2,500 depending on scope.
- Competitive national campaigns: £1,500 or more a month, which is usually overkill for a local business.
- Doing it yourself: mostly your time, plus £0–£100 a month for tools.

## What actually drives the price

The range above is wide for a reason. A village hairdresser and a solicitor in central Manchester are playing very different games. These are the things that move the number:

- **Competition:** ranking for 'accountant in London' takes far more work than 'accountant in Frome'.
- **Your starting point:** a fast, well-built website needs less fixing than a slow, dated one.
- **Scope:** are you targeting one town or fifteen, one service or a whole menu of them?
- **Content:** Google favours genuinely useful pages, and someone has to research and write them.
- **Ongoing versus one-off:** a single tidy-up is cheaper, but local rankings need feeding to hold their place.

## Freelancer, agency or doing it yourself?

A good freelancer is affordable and personal, but they are one person, so holidays, illness and busy spells all slow things down. A large agency has more hands, yet you can end up a small account passed to a junior. A small studio like ours aims to sit in the middle: enough people to keep things moving, few enough that you deal with us directly and we pick up the phone. Whichever route you pick, choose someone who understands your trade, because the way people search for a [dentist, builder or restaurant](/industries) is never quite the same.

You can also do the basics yourself: claim your Google Business Profile, gather honest reviews, and write clear pages about each service and area you cover. Plenty of local businesses win their first enquiries this way. Paid help earns its keep when the DIY route stalls, or when your time is simply worth more spent running the business.

## Why the cheapest SEO is often the dearest

When someone offers guaranteed number-one rankings for £99 a month, be careful. Nobody can guarantee rankings because Google decides those, and rock-bottom prices usually mean corners cut: spammy links that risk a penalty, thin copied content, or reports full of numbers that mean nothing. Undoing that damage costs far more than doing the job properly the first time.

- Guaranteed rankings or promises of overnight results.
- Long contracts with no clear list of what is actually being done.
- No access to your own Google Business Profile, analytics or website login.
- Reports heavy on jargon but silent on real enquiries and calls.

## Frequently asked questions

### How much does SEO cost for a small business in the UK?

There is no single price. Most UK local businesses sensibly spend between £300 and £1,200 a month for ongoing SEO, or a few hundred to a couple of thousand pounds for a well-scoped one-off project. We quote SEO per project after reviewing your site, so the figure fits your goals rather than a generic package. For context, our fixed-price websites start at £477 and care plans from £27 a month.

### How long does SEO take to work?

Honestly, months rather than days. For most local businesses you will start to see meaningful movement in three to six months, sometimes sooner for less competitive searches. Anyone promising overnight results is not being straight with you. A new website itself is quicker: most of our builds go live in three to six weeks.

### Is cheap SEO worth it?

Sometimes, and sometimes it is the most expensive mistake you can make. Cheap, honest work like gathering reviews and tidying your Google Business Profile is well worth it. Cheap, spammy work, such as bought links and copied content, can get your site penalised and cost far more to undo. Always ask exactly what you are paying for.

### Do I have to pay for SEO every month?

No. Ongoing retainers suit competitive markets, but many small businesses do well with a solid website, a strong Google Business Profile and a one-off improvement project, topped up occasionally. You own your site, domain and data with us, so you are never locked in.

### Can I do SEO myself?

Yes, the basics are very doable: claim your Google Business Profile, gather honest reviews, keep your site fast, and write clear pages for each service and area you cover. Bring in paid help when progress stalls or your time is better spent running the business.
