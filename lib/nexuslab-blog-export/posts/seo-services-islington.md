## How Islington customers actually search

Islington packs a lot of different worlds into a small borough. There's the buzz of Upper Street and Angel, the antique stalls of Camden Passage, the Georgian terraces of Barnsbury and Canonbury, the commuters and students around Highbury & Islington, and the creative and professional firms clustered down in Clerkenwell and around Exmouth Market. What connects them all is the phone. Almost nobody walks in cold any more. They search, skim a review or two, and decide, usually in under a minute.

Those searches are hyper-local. People don't type 'restaurant' or 'dentist', they type 'restaurant Upper Street', 'dentist Angel' or 'electrician N1'. A family in Highbury after an emergency plumber wants something different from a studio near Old Street hunting for an accountant. If your website and Google Business Profile don't make it obvious which of those areas you serve, you drop out of the running before the search has even finished.

## The map pack: Islington's most valuable shopfront

Search for almost anything local and Google shows a small map with three businesses pinned to it, right above the usual list of links. That's the map pack, and for a local business it's the most valuable strip of screen space on the internet. The paid ads sit above it, but the map pack is the first thing most people actually trust and tap.

Google decides who lands there using three broad things: relevance (how closely you match what was typed), distance (how near you are to the searcher) and prominence (how well known and trusted you look, largely through reviews and mentions elsewhere on the web). You can't shift your premises closer to everyone between Archway and Old Street, but relevance and prominence are far more in your hands than most owners realise.

There's no single trick to this. Ranking well across Islington comes from a handful of unglamorous jobs done consistently:
