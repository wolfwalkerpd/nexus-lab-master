## How people in Shoreditch actually search

Before you spend a penny on design, it helps to picture how someone really finds you. Most local searches happen on a phone, often while people are out and moving between meetings, markets and bars:

- Someone walking down Brick Lane types "barber near me" or "brunch near Old Street" and taps one of the first results on the map
- They scan your photos, opening hours and reviews before they read a single line of copy
- They give up in seconds if the site is slow, cramped on mobile or asks them to pinch and zoom
- They cross-check you against your Instagram — the two need to tell the same story
- Almost nobody reaches page two of Google, so [showing up in local search](/services/seo) matters as much as the design itself

## What goes into a site that brings in enquiries

A website that earns its keep here does a handful of unglamorous things well. It loads fast on a phone, because that is where nearly all your visitors are. It says what you do and where you are within the first screen, no scrolling required. It makes getting in touch effortless — tap to call, tap for directions, a booking link that actually works. And it feeds your Google Business Profile with accurate information, so you show up on the map when someone nearby is ready to spend rather than just browsing. None of this needs to be complicated. It needs to be done properly, by a team that has built local sites before and knows the small places where enquiries quietly leak away. That is the difference between a website that looks busy and one that brings you work.
