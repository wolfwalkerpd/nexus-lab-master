## What makes selling online in Brighton different

Brighton isn't a place where a generic template quietly blends in. This is a city of independents — the shops of The Lanes, the studios and cafes of North Laine, the bars along the seafront and the family businesses tucked into Hanover, Kemptown and Hove. Customers here have taste and they can spot a lazy website a mile off. If yours looks like everyone else's, you've already lost the click.

There's also a rhythm to the place. Summer brings day-trippers off the London train, festival crowds and tourists wandering the front with a phone out, hunting for the nearest good coffee or a table for tonight. Winter leans on regulars. A website built for Brighton has to work for both — fast on mobile, clear about where you are, and easy to act on. In the first few seconds, a visitor is quietly deciding:

- Where you are and whether you're easy to reach
- What you offer and whether it's for people like them
- Whether you look open, trusted and worth the trip
- How to book, buy or call without hunting for it
