A cheap website looks like a bargain right up until you add up what it truly costs you. The £4-a-month builder, the £99 marketplace special, the friend's cousin who 'does a bit of web design' — the upfront number is easy to say yes to. But the price of a website is never just the build fee. It's the enquiries that never land, the evenings you lose fiddling with a template, and the rebuild you end up paying for a year down the line.

So let's be honest about cheap website vs professional website, and the real cost of each in plain UK numbers. No scare tactics and no hard sell — just where the money actually goes, what you get for it, and how to tell a fair price from a false economy before you hand over your card.

## What a cheap website actually costs

The sticker price on a cheap website is rarely the real one. A DIY builder starts at a few pounds a month, but the true bill shows up in your own time and in the sales you quietly miss. Here's where the hidden cost hides:

- **Your hours.** A weekend building it yourself, then an evening every month keeping it patched, is time you could spend running your business.
- **Lost enquiries.** A slow, cluttered or hard-to-use site turns visitors away before they ever call. You never see those lost customers, so the cost feels invisible.
- **Add-ons that creep up.** The £5 plan becomes £20 once you add a contact form, remove the ads, connect a domain and bolt on 'premium' features.
- **The rebuild.** Cheap sites are built to sell, not to last. Most need replacing within a year or two, so you pay for the whole thing twice.
- **Lock-in.** Many budget platforms keep hold of your content. Leave, and you often can't take the site, the design or sometimes even the domain with you.

## Cheap website vs professional website: the real cost side by side

Put the two next to each other over three years, not three months, and the picture changes. A cheap website wins on day one and loses by month twelve. A professional site costs more upfront and then quietly earns its keep.

- **Cheap:** low upfront price, a generic template, your own time to build and maintain, thin or no support, and a likely rebuild within eighteen months.
- **Professional:** a higher fixed price, a site designed around how your customers actually search and buy, built to load fast and convert, with real people to call when something breaks.
- **The tie-breaker:** one extra job a month from a site that works usually covers the difference many times over. For a tradesperson or a dental practice, a single new customer can be worth more than the whole build.

## What actually drives the price of a website

Price varies wildly because 'a website' can mean five pages of clear, well-written content or a fifty-page booking system. When you get a quote, these are the things moving the number up or down:

- **Number of pages and features** — a booking form, online payments or a members' area all add real work.
- **Copywriting and photography** — words and images that sell take time; stock and filler do not, and it shows.
- **Design from scratch vs a template** — a look built around your brand costs more than a theme with your logo dropped on top.
- **[SEO](/services/seo) and local visibility** — being found on Google is a job in its own right, separate from the build.
- **Ongoing care** — hosting, updates, backups and small tweaks, usually covered by a monthly [care plan](/services/care-plans).

## Where a professional website pays for itself

A professional site isn't about looking flashy. It's about doing a job: turning the people who find you into people who contact you. Good [website design](/services/website-design) loads in a couple of seconds, reads clearly on a phone — where most local searches happen — and makes the next step obvious. That's where the return lives.

It also saves you the hidden costs above. You're not the one patching it late at night, you're not losing enquiries to a clunky form, and you're not paying to rebuild the whole thing next spring. Spend a little more once, and you stop bleeding money in ways you can't easily see.

> A cheap website saves you money once. A professional one earns it back every month it's live.
