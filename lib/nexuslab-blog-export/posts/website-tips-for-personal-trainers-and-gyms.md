If you run a gym or train clients one to one, your website really has one job: turn a curious visitor into a booked session. Most of the website tips for personal trainers and gyms you'll find online forget that. They obsess over slick animations and stock photos of dumbbells, when what actually fills your timetable is a clear offer, an easy booking step and honest proof that you get people results.

Here are the changes that make the biggest difference to a fitness website, in plain English, plus how to spot the ones quietly costing you enquiries right now.

## Put booking front and centre

When someone lands on your site, they've usually already decided they want to train. They're just checking you're the right fit and easy to reach, so don't make them hunt. Your main action, whether that's a free consultation, a taster class or a quick call, should sit right at the top of the page and follow them as they scroll down.

Good [website design](/services/website-design) makes that next step obvious on every page, not buried three clicks deep. Put your phone number where a thumb can reach it, keep forms short (name, email and what they're after), and if you use a booking tool, link straight to it rather than asking people to email and wait for a reply.

## The pages every fitness website needs

You don't need twenty pages. You need a handful that answer what a new member is actually thinking. Before you add anything clever, make sure you've nailed the essentials:

Get these right and most visitors will have everything they need to say yes.

- A clear services or **membership** page that lists what you offer and roughly what it costs
- A simple booking or enquiry step that works in a couple of taps
- An about page with real photos of you and your space, not stock imagery
- A timetable or class schedule people can actually read on a phone
- Genuine reviews and results from members who have trained with you

## Don't hide your prices

Fitness is one of the few trades where hiding prices is still normal, and it quietly costs you good enquiries. People assume the worst, or they simply move on to a competitor who is upfront. You don't have to publish every package or session rate, but give a starting point, a range or a clear 'from' figure so visitors can picture whether you're the right fit before they get in touch.

The trainers who do this well tend to hear from warmer, better-qualified people who are already close to booking, rather than tyre-kickers fishing for a number. Being open about money builds trust before you've even spoken.

## Website tips for personal trainers and gyms that help you get found

A beautiful site nobody sees won't fill your classes. When someone nearby searches for a personal trainer or a gym in your area, you want to be one of the names they see, and most of those searches happen on a phone, sometimes while they're walking past your door.

That comes down to local SEO: a fully filled-out Google Business Profile, the same name, address and phone number everywhere online, real reviews, and pages written around what people actually type in. It's worth investing in proper [local SEO](/services/seo) once your site is solid, because it keeps working long after the launch buzz fades.

> If your gym doesn't show up when someone searches for a trainer nearby, it doesn't matter how polished your website looks.

## Show proof, without the hype

People are trusting you with their body and their time, so proof matters more than promises. Skip the guaranteed-results language and let real members do the talking. A few honest testimonials, genuine before-and-after photos you have permission to use, and clear detail about your qualifications will beat a page of superlatives every time.

If you're just starting out and don't have testimonials yet, be upfront about it. Show your training style, your space and what a first session actually looks like. People warm to honesty far more quickly than they warm to hype.

## Keep it fast, mobile and up to date

Most people will open your site on a phone between other things, so it has to load quickly and read well on a small screen. A slow, cluttered site loses enquiries you'll never even hear about. Compress your images, keep the layout simple, and test every page on your own phone before you call it finished.

Your timetable, prices and offers change, so you need to update them without paying someone each time. A simple [care plan](/services/care-plans) keeps your site fast, secure and current, and it's worth checking our [pricing](/pricing) so you know the cost up front instead of guessing. ## The quick wins

- Make booking or enquiring the single most obvious thing on every page.
- Be upfront about prices to attract warmer, better-qualified enquiries.
- Show up locally with a strong Google Business Profile and honest reviews.
- Keep it fast and mobile-friendly, and make sure you can update it yourself.
