Here's the part a lot of agencies won't tell you: plenty of sites don't need tearing down. If the structure underneath is sound, we can often keep what works and fix what doesn't — a fresh design, faster pages, clearer wording and a proper mobile layout — without the cost and upheaval of a complete rebuild. Sometimes a redesign really is just that: a redesign.

Other times the foundations are the real problem — a platform that's slow, insecure or impossible to edit — and starting fresh is honestly the cheaper path over a couple of years.
