## How people in Cardiff search for what you sell

Most local searches happen on a mobile, often while someone is already out and about. Picture a visitor on Queen Street looking for lunch, a parent in Whitchurch searching for a dentist that takes new patients, or a landlord in Cathays who needs an electrician today. They tap in a few words, glance at the top results and pick one. If your site is slow, hard to read on a phone or missing the one thing they wanted to know, they are back on the results page and on to your competitor within seconds.

Cardiff also has its own rhythm. On a Principality Stadium match day the city fills up and searches spike for food, drink and parking. Term time brings tens of thousands of students hunting for services near Cathays and the city centre. A website that loads fast and answers the obvious question wins that moment. One that makes people pinch and zoom loses it.

- Whether you are open and how to reach you — a tap-to-call number, not a form buried three clicks deep
- Where you are and whether you cover their area, from Grangetown to Cyncoed
- Proof you have done this before: real photos, honest reviews, and a clear price or starting point
- One obvious next step, whether that is booking, calling or requesting a quote

## What good web design in Cardiff looks like

Good [web design in Cardiff](/services/website-design) starts with the customer, not the graphics. Before we choose a single colour or font, we work out who is landing on the page, what they need, and what you want them to do next. Everything else follows from that.

In practice that means a site that loads quickly on a mid-range phone on 4G, headlines that say what you do and where in plain English, and a clear way to get in touch on every page. It means content organised the way your customers think, not the way your business is structured internally. And it means your strongest selling points — your reviews, your guarantee, your prices — sitting where people will actually see them.

> A pretty website that nobody can find or use is just an expensive business card.

## Get found across Cardiff's neighbourhoods with local SEO

Cardiff is really a collection of distinct areas, and people search that way. Someone types 'barber Pontcanna' or 'accountant Cardiff Bay', not just 'barber'. Ranking for those searches is the job of [local SEO](/services/seo): a Google Business Profile that is properly filled in, pages that mention the areas you genuinely serve, and consistent name, address and phone details wherever you are listed online.

You do not need to fake a presence in every postcode. It works far better to be honest about where you operate — whether that is a single shop on Wellfield Road or a trades business covering the whole of South Wales — and to make it crystal clear. Google rewards relevance and clarity, and so do customers.

- A complete, accurate Google Business Profile with real photos and up-to-date hours
- Pages that name the neighbourhoods and services you actually cover
- The same business name, address and phone number everywhere online
- Genuine reviews from real customers, replied to like a human
