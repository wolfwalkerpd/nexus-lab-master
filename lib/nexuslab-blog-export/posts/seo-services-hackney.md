## Why the map pack decides who gets the call

Most people here search on their phones, on the move — walking down Kingsland Road, waiting for the Overground at Hackney Central, or deciding where to eat after a wander round Netil Market. They type something local, tap one of the first results, and rarely scroll past the map. Because most local searches happen on mobile, that map pack sits right at the top of the screen, above the ordinary blue links.

The three businesses inside it collect the calls, the bookings and the walk-ins. Everyone below is, in practice, invisible — even if their website is lovely. That's why local SEO is less about vanity and more about survival for an independent competing on Mare Street or Chatsworth Road.

## What SEO services in Hackney actually involve

Good [local SEO](/services/seo) isn't one clever trick. It's a stack of small, honest signals that tell Google you're a real, relevant, trusted business in this area. The ones that matter most are:

- A complete, active Google Business Profile — the single biggest factor in whether you appear in the map pack.
- Consistent name, address and phone number everywhere your business is listed online.
- Genuine reviews, earned steadily over time, that mention what you do and where.
- Pages on your own site written for real Hackney searches, not vague national keywords.
- A fast website that works properly on a phone, where nearly all of these searches happen.
- A handful of relevant local links — a Broadway Market trader listing, a supplier, a local directory.

## Start with your Google Business Profile

If you only fix one thing this month, make it your Google Business Profile. It's free, it's yours, and it's the strongest single signal for the map pack. Choose the most accurate primary category, add real photos of your shop, your staff or your finished work, and keep your opening hours honest — nothing frustrates a customer more than a wasted trip to a closed door.

Set the areas you cover: Dalston, Clapton, Homerton, De Beauvoir Town, wherever your customers actually come from. Then ask happy customers for reviews, steadily, and reply to every one, good or bad. Google reads all of this as proof you're active and legitimate, and it's usually the fastest lever a Hackney business has.

## Your website still does the heavy lifting

Your profile gets you seen; your [website](/services/website-design) turns that attention into enquiries. It needs to load quickly, read clearly on a small screen, and answer the questions a local customer actually has — what you do, where you are, what it costs and how to book. Service and area pages that genuinely speak to Clapton, Stoke Newington or Hackney Wick help Google connect you to those searches, as long as they're written for people rather than stuffed with keywords. Thin, generic pages do more harm than good.

A site that's fast today can slow down as photos and plugins pile up, so it's worth keeping it maintained with a simple [care plan](/services/care-plans). That way your speed, security and content don't quietly rot over the course of a year.

## Where Hackney businesses go wrong

We see the same handful of mistakes over and over in a teardown. Fixing them rarely needs a full rebuild — just attention to the basics.

- A half-finished Business Profile with no photos, the wrong category, or hours that haven't been touched in a year.
- The business name, address and phone written three different ways across Google, Facebook and old directories.
- A homepage that never actually says which part of London it serves.
- A site that's fine on a laptop but painful to use on the phone, where the customers are.
- No recent reviews, or reviews left sitting without a single reply.
