## What Camden customers do before they visit

Camden is really several places at once. There's the tourist crush around Camden Lock and the markets, the settled, well-off streets of Primrose Hill and Hampstead, the students around UCL and Bloomsbury, and the growing cluster of media and tech firms up by King's Cross and St Pancras. What ties them together is the phone. Almost nobody wanders in cold any more. They search, they compare, they skim a couple of reviews, and then they decide, often in under a minute. If your site is slow, awkward on a small screen, or missing the one thing they wanted to know, such as your opening hours or whether you take walk-ins, they are gone. The next business is one tap away.

## Where a lot of Camden websites fall short

Walk down Camden High Street and you'll pass shops and cafés that are heaving in person yet almost invisible online. The same problems repeat: a site built years ago that pinches and zooms on a phone, no clear address or opening hours, a contact form nobody checks, stock photos instead of the real place, and text so stuffed with keywords it reads like a robot wrote it. Any one of these quietly costs you customers. Putting them right isn't about a flashy redesign for its own sake. It's about removing every reason for someone to give up and tap the next result.
